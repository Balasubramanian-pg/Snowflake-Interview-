# What are the Restrictions in Section 26.3

> [!TIP]
> Section 26.3 outlines specific limitations and constraints that must be adhered to in order to ensure compliance and avoid penalties.

## Why This Matters
- Understanding the restrictions in Section 26.3 is crucial for maintaining regulatory compliance and avoiding legal repercussions.
- Failure to comply with these restrictions can result in significant financial penalties and damage to one's reputation.
- This knowledge is essential for individuals and organizations operating within the scope of Section 26.3, particularly when engaging in activities that may be subject to these restrictions.

## Key Points
- The restrictions in Section 26.3 apply to all entities operating within the specified jurisdiction or industry.
- These restrictions include limitations on certain activities, such as data collection and storage, and impose specific requirements for transparency and disclosure.
- Compliance with these restrictions requires a thorough understanding of the regulatory framework and the implementation of effective policies and procedures.

> [!important]
> It is mandatory to conduct a thorough risk assessment and obtain necessary approvals before engaging in any activity that may be subject to the restrictions outlined in Section 26.3.

- Identify the specific restrictions applicable to your entity → Assess the risks associated with non-compliance → Implement policies and procedures to ensure compliance.
- Or: Regulatory requirement → Risk assessment → Compliance implementation → Ongoing monitoring and review.

## Gotchas
- Assuming that the restrictions in Section 26.3 do not apply to your entity or activity, when in fact they do.
- Failing to conduct a thorough risk assessment, which can lead to non-compliance and penalties.
- Overlooking the need for ongoing monitoring and review to ensure continued compliance with the restrictions.

> [!NOTE]
> For example, a company operating in the healthcare industry must ensure that it complies with the restrictions on data collection and storage outlined in Section 26.3, or face significant penalties and reputational damage.
