# 85.5. Averages
> The concept of averages is crucial for summarizing and analyzing data, as it provides a single value that represents the central tendency of a dataset.

  I'll create a comprehensive guide to explain **Averages** in Snowflake using **pharmaceutical data** (claims, formulary, LAD, retail sales, 840/852/837 EDI transactions). Averages are central tendency measures that summarize data distributions—critical for drug pricing analysis, claims processing metrics, and formulary decision-making.

## What are Averages in Pharma Data Analysis?

In pharmaceutical analytics, **averages** (mean, median, mode) provide single-value summaries of complex datasets. Unlike the programming concept of slices/windows, averages are **aggregative statistics** that reduce dimensional data to representative values—essential for:
- **Drug pricing benchmarks** (average wholesale price, average sales price)
- **Claims processing efficiency** (average adjudication time, average claim value)
- **Formulary performance** (average rebate rates, average utilization)
- **Patient outcomes** (average medication adherence, average therapy duration)

## Use Case 1: Drug Pricing Analysis - Mean vs. Median (Formulary)

**Scenario**: Compare mean vs. median drug prices to identify pricing outliers and market skew.

```sql
-- Create formulary pricing data with outliers (specialty drugs)
CREATE OR REPLACE TABLE formulary_pricing (
    ndc_code VARCHAR(11),
    drug_name VARCHAR(100),
    therapeutic_class VARCHAR(50),
    dosage_form VARCHAR(30),
    strength VARCHAR(20),
    awp_unit_price DECIMAL(10,4), -- Average Wholesale Price
    asp_unit_price DECIMAL(10,4), -- Average Sales Price
    wac_unit_price DECIMAL(10,4), -- Wholesale Acquisition Cost
    contract_tier INTEGER,
    volume_tier VARCHAR(20),
    effective_date DATE
);

-- Insert pricing data with extreme outliers (specialty vs. generic)
INSERT INTO formulary_pricing VALUES
-- Generic drugs (low price, high volume)
('00093-1058-01', 'Lisinopril 10mg', 'ACE Inhibitors', 'Tablet', '10mg', 0.4500, 0.3800, 0.3200, 1, 'high_volume', '2024-01-01'),

('00093-2748-01', 'Metformin 500mg', 'Antidiabetic', 'Tablet', '500mg', 0.7500, 0.6200, 0.5500, 1, 'high_volume', '2024-01-01'),
('00093-2748-02', 'Metformin 1000mg', 'Antidiabetic', 'Tablet', '1000mg', 0.8900, 0.7400, 0.6500, 1, 'high_volume', '2024-01-01'),

('00555-0808-02', 'Atorvastatin 20mg', 'Statins', 'Tablet', '20mg', 1.4500, 1.2500, 1.1000, 1, 'high_volume', '2024-01-01'),
('00555-0808-03', 'Atorvastatin 40mg', 'Statins', 'Tablet', '40mg', 1.8500, 1.5800, 1.4000, 1, 'high_volume', '2024-01-01'),

-- Branded drugs (medium price)

-- Specialty drugs (extreme outliers - high price, low volume)
('61755-001-01', 'Revlimid 5mg', 'Immunomodulators', 'Capsule', '5mg', 850.0000, 765.0000, 720.0000, 3, 'low_volume', '2024-01-01'),

('61755-001-03', 'Revlimid 25mg', 'Immunomodulators', 'Capsule', '25mg', 1850.0000, 1665.0000, 1570.0000, 3, 'low_volume', '2024-01-01'),
('00078-0429-61', 'Harvoni', 'Antivirals', 'Tablet', '90mg/400mg', 1200.0000, 1125.0000, 1050.0000, 3, 'low_volume', '2024-01-01'),
('00078-0430-61', 'Sovaldi', 'Antivirals', 'Tablet', '400mg', 1100.0000, 1000.0000, 950.0000, 3, 'low_volume', '2024-01-01');

-- AVERAGES QUERY: Mean vs. Median vs. Mode analysis
WITH price_statistics AS (
    SELECT 
        therapeutic_class,
        
        -- Mean averages (sensitive to outliers)
        ROUND(AVG(awp_unit_price), 4) AS mean_awp,
        ROUND(AVG(asp_unit_price), 4) AS mean_asp,
        ROUND(AVG(wac_unit_price), 4) AS mean_wac,
        
        -- Median averages (robust to outliers)
        MEDIAN(awp_unit_price) AS median_awp,
        MEDIAN(asp_unit_price) AS median_asp,
        MEDIAN(wac_unit_price) AS median_wac,
        
        -- Mode averages (most frequent price points)
        MODE(awp_unit_price) AS mode_awp,
        MODE(asp_unit_price) AS mode_asp,
        
        -- Distribution spread (detecting skew)
        STDDEV(awp_unit_price) AS stddev_awp,
        MIN(awp_unit_price) AS min_awp,
        MAX(awp_unit_price) AS max_awp,
        MAX(awp_unit_price) - MIN(awp_unit_price) AS price_range,
        
        -- Count for context
        COUNT(*) AS drug_count
        
    FROM formulary_pricing
    
    GROUP BY therapeutic_class
),
outlier_detection AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Skew detection: Mean significantly different from Median indicates outliers
        CASE 
            WHEN mean_awp > median_awp * 2 THEN 'HIGH_RIGHT_SKEW_OUTLIERS_PRESENT'
            WHEN mean_awp < median_awp * 0.5 THEN 'LEFT_SKEW'
            WHEN ABS(mean_awp - median_awp) / NULLIF(median_awp, 0) > 0.5 THEN 'MODERATE_SKEW'
            ELSE 'SYMMETRIC_DISTRIBUTION'
        END AS distribution_shape,
        
        -- Outlier impact quantification
        ROUND((mean_awp - median_awp) / NULLIF(median_awp, 0) * 100, 2) AS mean_median_variance_pct,
        
        -- Coefficient of variation (relative spread)
        ROUND(stddev_awp / NULLIF(mean_awp, 0) * 100, 2) AS coefficient_of_variation,
        
        -- Price tier classification based on averages
        CASE 
            WHEN median_awp < 1.00 THEN 'TIER_1_GENERIC'
            WHEN median_awp < 20.00 THEN 'TIER_2_BRANDED'
            ELSE 'TIER_3_SPECIALTY'
        END AS price_tier_by_median,
        
        -- Warning: Using mean for tier classification would misclassify
        CASE 
            WHEN mean_awp < 1.00 THEN 'TIER_1_GENERIC_BY_MEAN'
            WHEN mean_awp < 20.00 THEN 'TIER_2_BRANDED_BY_MEAN'
            ELSE 'TIER_3_SPECIALTY_BY_MEAN'
        END AS misclassification_risk
        
    FROM price_statistics
)
SELECT 
    therapeutic_class,
    drug_count,
    
    -- Mean averages (with outlier warning)
    mean_awp AS mean_price,
    mean_asp AS mean_sales_price,
    
    -- Median averages (robust measure)
    median_awp AS median_price,
    median_asp AS median_sales_price,
    
    -- Mode averages (market concentration)
    mode_awp AS most_common_price,
    
    -- Critical comparison: Mean vs Median
    ROUND(mean_awp, 2) AS mean_for_reporting,
    ROUND(median_awp, 2) AS median_for_benchmarking,
    mean_median_variance_pct AS outlier_impact_percentage,
    
    -- Distribution analysis
    min_awp AS lowest_price,
    max_awp AS highest_price,
    price_range,
    stddev_awp AS price_volatility,
    coefficient_of_variation AS relative_volatility_pct,
    
    -- Data quality assessment
    distribution_shape,
    price_tier_by_median AS correct_tier_classification,
    misclassification_risk AS incorrect_tier_if_using_mean,
    
    -- Business insight: Which average to use?
    CASE 
        WHEN distribution_shape = 'HIGH_RIGHT_SKEW_OUTLIERS_PRESENT' 
        THEN 'USE_MEDIAN_FOR_CONTRACTS_MEAN_WILL_OVERESTIMATE'
        WHEN coefficient_of_variation < 30 
        THEN 'USE_MEAN_DISTRIBUTION_IS_STABLE'
        ELSE 'USE_MEDIAN_AND_MEAN_TOGETHER_FOR_CONTEXT'
    END AS recommended_average_for_negotiations,
    
    -- Silent failure warning
    CASE 
        WHEN mean_awp > 100 AND median_awp < 10 
        THEN 'WARNING_MEAN_MASKS_AFFORDABILITY_CRISIS'
        ELSE 'AVERAGES_ARE_REPRESENTATIVE'
    END AS data_interpretation_alert
    
FROM outlier_detection

ORDER BY 
    CASE therapeutic_class 
        WHEN 'ACE Inhibitors' THEN 1
        WHEN 'Antidiabetic' THEN 2
        WHEN 'Statins' THEN 3
        WHEN 'DPP-4 Inhibitors' THEN 4
        WHEN 'Immunomodulators' THEN 5
        ELSE 6
    END;
```

**Workflow Explanation**:

1. **Mean Calculation** - `AVG()` calculates arithmetic mean (sensitive to $1,850 specialty drug outliers)
2. **Median Calculation** - `MEDIAN()` finds middle value (robust measure for skewed distributions)
3. **Mode Calculation** - `MODE()` identifies most frequent price (market concentration indicator)
4. **Skew Detection** - Compares mean vs median to detect outlier influence (like the $50K salary example with executives)
5. **Business Recommendation** - Recommends which average to use for contract negotiations based on distribution shape

## Use Case 2: Claims Processing Efficiency - Weighted Averages (837)

**Scenario**: Calculate weighted average adjudication times by claim volume to properly resource planning.

```sql
-- Create EDI 837 claims processing data
CREATE OR REPLACE TABLE claims_processing (
    claim_id VARCHAR(30),
    submitter_id VARCHAR(20),
    payer_id VARCHAR(20),
    claim_type VARCHAR(10), -- 'PROF' (professional) or 'INST' (institutional)
    claim_complexity VARCHAR(20), -- 'simple', 'moderate', 'complex'
    line_item_count INTEGER,
    billed_amount DECIMAL(10,2),
    submission_timestamp TIMESTAMP,
    adjudication_timestamp TIMESTAMP,
    processing_time_minutes INTEGER,
    auto_adjudicated BOOLEAN,
    denial_reason VARCHAR(50)
);

-- Insert claims with varying complexity and processing times
INSERT INTO claims_processing VALUES
-- Simple claims (high volume, fast processing)
('CLM_001', 'PHARM_001', 'PAYER_A', 'PROF', 'simple', 1, 45.00, '2024-03-01 09:00:00', '2024-03-01 09:05:00', 5, TRUE, NULL),
('CLM_002', 'PHARM_001', 'PAYER_A', 'PROF', 'simple', 1, 52.00, '2024-03-01 09:01:00', '2024-03-01 09:06:00', 5, TRUE, NULL),
('CLM_003', 'PHARM_001', 'PAYER_A', 'PROF', 'simple', 2, 89.00, '2024-03-01 09:02:00', '2024-03-01 09:08:00', 6, TRUE, NULL),
('CLM_004', 'PHARM_002', 'PAYER_A', 'PROF', 'simple', 1, 34.00, '2024-03-01 09:03:00', '2024-03-01 09:08:00', 5, TRUE, NULL),
('CLM_005', 'PHARM_002', 'PAYER_A', 'PROF', 'simple', 1, 67.00, '2024-03-01 09:04:00', '2024-03-01 09:10:00', 6, TRUE, NULL),

-- Moderate complexity (medium volume, medium processing)
('CLM_006', 'HOSP_001', 'PAYER_A', 'INST', 'moderate', 15, 1250.00, '2024-03-01 09:10:00', '2024-03-01 09:45:00', 35, FALSE, NULL),
('CLM_007', 'HOSP_001', 'PAYER_A', 'INST', 'moderate', 22, 3400.00, '2024-03-01 09:15:00', '2024-03-01 09:55:00', 40, FALSE, NULL),
('CLM_008', 'CLIN_001', 'PAYER_B', 'PROF', 'moderate', 8, 450.00, '2024-03-01 09:20:00', '2024-03-01 09:50:00', 30, FALSE, NULL),

-- Complex claims (low volume, very slow processing - outliers)
('CLM_009', 'HOSP_002', 'PAYER_B', 'INST', 'complex', 45, 12500.00, '2024-03-01 08:00:00', '2024-03-01 16:00:00', 480, FALSE, 'MANUAL_REVIEW_REQUIRED'),
('CLM_010', 'HOSP_002', 'PAYER_B', 'INST', 'complex', 52, 28000.00, '2024-03-01 08:30:00', '2024-03-01 17:30:00', 540, FALSE, 'PENDED_FOR_MEDICAL_REVIEW'),
('CLM_011', 'SPEC_001', 'PAYER_C', 'INST', 'complex', 38, 8900.00, '2024-03-01 09:00:00', '2024-03-01 15:30:00', 390, FALSE, 'COORDINATION_OF_BENEFITS');

-- AVERAGES QUERY: Simple vs. Weighted vs. Adjusted averages
WITH processing_stats AS (
    SELECT 
        payer_id,
        claim_complexity,
        
        -- Simple averages (unweighted - each claim counts equally)
        AVG(processing_time_minutes) AS simple_avg_processing_time,
        AVG(billed_amount) AS simple_avg_claim_value,
        
        -- Weighted averages by volume (accounts for claim frequency)
        -- Weighted average = SUM(value * weight) / SUM(weight)
        SUM(processing_time_minutes * 1.0) / COUNT(*) AS weighted_by_count_time,
        
        -- Weighted by claim value (high-value claims get more weight)
        SUM(processing_time_minutes * billed_amount) / SUM(billed_amount) AS value_weighted_time,
        
        -- Weighted by line items (complexity-weighted)
        SUM(processing_time_minutes * line_item_count) / SUM(line_item_count) AS complexity_weighted_time,
        
        -- Median for robust measure
        MEDIAN(processing_time_minutes) AS median_processing_time,
        
        -- Mode (most common processing bucket)
        MODE(CASE 
            WHEN processing_time_minutes <= 10 THEN '0-10_min'
            WHEN processing_time_minutes <= 30 THEN '11-30_min'
            WHEN processing_time_minutes <= 60 THEN '31-60_min'
            ELSE '60+_min'
        END) AS mode_time_bucket,
        
        -- Distribution metrics
        STDDEV(processing_time_minutes) AS time_stddev,
        MIN(processing_time_minutes) AS min_time,
        MAX(processing_time_minutes) AS max_time,
        
        -- Volume metrics
        COUNT(*) AS claim_volume,
        SUM(billed_amount) AS total_value,
        SUM(line_item_count) AS total_line_items,
        SUM(CASE WHEN auto_adjudicated THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS auto_adjudicated_count
        
    FROM claims_processing
    
    GROUP BY payer_id, claim_complexity
),
efficiency_analysis AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Auto-adjudication rate (efficiency metric)
        ROUND(auto_adjudicated_count * 100.0 / claim_volume, 2) AS auto_adjudication_rate,
        
        -- Outlier impact: Simple avg vs Weighted avg
        ROUND((simple_avg_processing_time - weighted_by_count_time) / 
              NULLIF(weighted_by_count_time, 0) * 100, 2) AS unweighted_bias_pct,
        
        -- Value concentration: Are high-value claims taking longer?
        ROUND((value_weighted_time - simple_avg_processing_time) / 
              NULLIF(simple_avg_processing_time, 0) * 100, 2) AS high_value_time_premium_pct,
        
        -- Silent failure detection: Mean masks operational reality
        CASE 
            WHEN simple_avg_processing_time > 60 AND median_processing_time < 15 
            THEN 'MEAN_MASKS_EFFICIENCY_PROBLEM'
            WHEN simple_avg_processing_time > 100 
            THEN 'EXTREME_OUTLIER_DISTORTION'
            ELSE 'AVERAGES_ALIGNED'
        END AS operational_reality_check,
        
        -- Resource planning recommendation
        CASE 
            WHEN claim_volume > 100 AND simple_avg_processing_time > 30 
            THEN 'HIGH_VOLUME_SLOW_PROCESSING_BOTTLENECK'
            WHEN value_weighted_time > simple_avg_processing_time * 1.5 
            THEN 'HIGH_VALUE_CLAIMS_DELAYED_RISK'
            WHEN auto_adjudication_rate < 50 AND claim_volume > 50 
            THEN 'AUTOMATION_OPPORTUNITY'
            ELSE 'PROCESSING_EFFICIENT'
        END AS resource_planning_insight
        
    FROM processing_stats
)
SELECT 
    payer_id,
    claim_complexity,
    claim_volume,
    
    -- Processing time averages comparison
    simple_avg_processing_time AS unweighted_avg_minutes,
    weighted_by_count_time AS volume_weighted_avg,
    value_weighted_time AS dollar_weighted_avg,
    complexity_weighted_time AS complexity_weighted_avg,
    median_processing_time AS robust_median_minutes,
    
    -- Critical insight: Which average represents reality?
    ROUND(simple_avg_processing_time, 1) AS reported_avg_processing_time,
    ROUND(median_processing_time, 1) AS actual_typical_experience,
    
    -- Financial context
    simple_avg_claim_value AS avg_claim_value,
    total_value AS total_claims_value,
    auto_adjudication_rate AS pct_auto_processed,
    
    -- Distribution spread
    min_time AS fastest_claim_minutes,
    max_time AS slowest_claim_minutes,
    time_stddev AS processing_variability,
    
    -- Business intelligence
    unweighted_bias_pct AS outlier_distortion_percentage,
    high_value_time_premium_pct AS value_processing_premium,
    operational_reality_check AS data_quality_alert,
    resource_planning_insight AS operational_recommendation,
    
    -- Correct average selection guide
    CASE 
        WHEN operational_reality_check = 'MEAN_MASKS_EFFICIENCY_PROBLEM' 
        THEN 'USE_MEDIAN_FOR_SLA_REPORTING'
        WHEN high_value_time_premium_pct > 50 
        THEN 'USE_VALUE_WEIGHTED_FOR_CAPACITY_PLANNING'
        WHEN claim_volume > 1000 
        THEN 'USE_VOLUME_WEIGHTED_FOR_STAFFING'
        ELSE 'SIMPLE_AVG_SUFFICIENT'
    END AS recommended_metric_for_kpis
    
FROM efficiency_analysis

ORDER BY payer_id, 
    CASE claim_complexity 
        WHEN 'simple' THEN 1 
        WHEN 'moderate' THEN 2 
        ELSE 3 
    END;
```

**Workflow Explanation**:

1. **Simple Average** - `AVG()` treats all claims equally (distorted by 540-minute complex claims)
2. **Volume-Weighted** - Accounts for claim frequency (simple claims dominate volume)
3. **Value-Weighted** - Weights by claim dollar amount (high-value claims get priority weight)
4. **Complexity-Weighted** - Weights by line items (true operational complexity measure)
5. **Operational Reality Check** - Detects when mean masks that 90% of claims process in <10 minutes

## Use Case 3: Retail Pharmacy Sales - Moving Averages (Retail Sales)

**Scenario**: Calculate 7-day and 30-day moving averages for inventory planning and trend detection.

```sql
-- Create daily retail sales data
CREATE OR REPLACE TABLE daily_pharmacy_sales (
    sale_date DATE,
    pharmacy_npi VARCHAR(10),
    ndc_code VARCHAR(11),
    units_sold INTEGER,
    revenue DECIMAL(10,2),
    prescription_count INTEGER,
    new_prescription_count INTEGER,
    refill_prescription_count INTEGER,
    generic_substitution_rate DECIMAL(5,2)
);

-- Insert 90 days of sales data with trends and seasonality
INSERT INTO daily_pharmacy_sales 
WITH date_series AS (
    SELECT DATEADD(DAY, -seq, CURRENT_DATE()) AS sale_date
    FROM TABLE(GENERATOR(ROWCOUNT => 90)) AS gen(seq)
),
products AS (
    SELECT '00093-1058-01' AS ndc, 'Lisinopril' AS name, 0.45 AS price UNION ALL
    SELECT '00093-2748-01', 'Metformin', 0.75 UNION ALL
    SELECT '00555-0808-02', 'Atorvastatin', 1.45
)
SELECT 
    ds.sale_date,
    'PHARM001' AS pharmacy_npi,
    p.ndc AS ndc_code,
    -- Simulate trend: increasing sales + weekly seasonality (higher on Mon/Fri)
    FLOOR(50 + (90 - DATEDIFF(DAY, ds.sale_date, CURRENT_DATE())) * 0.5 + 
          CASE WHEN DAYOFWEEK(ds.sale_date) IN (0, 5) THEN 20 ELSE 0 END + 
          UNIFORM(-10, 10, RANDOM())) AS units_sold,
    FLOOR(50 + (90 - DATEDIFF(DAY, ds.sale_date, CURRENT_DATE())) * 0.5 + 
          CASE WHEN DAYOFWEEK(ds.sale_date) IN (0, 5) THEN 20 ELSE 0 END + 
          UNIFORM(-10, 10, RANDOM())) * p.price AS revenue,
    FLOOR(30 + (90 - DATEDIFF(DAY, ds.sale_date, CURRENT_DATE())) * 0.3 + 
          UNIFORM(-5, 5, RANDOM())) AS prescription_count,
    FLOOR(10 + UNIFORM(-3, 3, RANDOM())) AS new_prescription_count,
    FLOOR(20 + (90 - DATEDIFF(DAY, ds.sale_date, CURRENT_DATE())) * 0.3 + 
          UNIFORM(-5, 5, RANDOM())) AS refill_prescription_count,
    0.75 + (UNIFORM(-5, 5, RANDOM()) / 100.0) AS generic_substitution_rate
FROM date_series ds
CROSS JOIN products p;

-- AVERAGES QUERY: Moving averages and trend analysis
WITH daily_aggregates AS (
    -- Aggregate to daily totals across all products
    SELECT 
        sale_date,
        pharmacy_npi,
        SUM(units_sold) AS daily_units,
        SUM(revenue) AS daily_revenue,
        SUM(prescription_count) AS daily_rx_count,
        AVG(generic_substitution_rate) AS daily_generic_rate,
        
        -- Day of week for seasonality analysis
        DAYOFWEEK(sale_date) AS day_of_week,
        DAYNAME(sale_date) AS day_name
        
    FROM daily_pharmacy_sales
    
    GROUP BY sale_date, pharmacy_npi
),
moving_calculations AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- 7-day moving average (short-term trend)
        AVG(daily_units) OVER (
            ORDER BY sale_date 
            ROWS BETWEEN 6 PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW
        ) AS ma_7_day_units,
        
        AVG(daily_revenue) OVER (
            ORDER BY sale_date 
            ROWS BETWEEN 6 PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW
        ) AS ma_7_day_revenue,
        
        -- 30-day moving average (long-term trend)
        AVG(daily_units) OVER (
            ORDER BY sale_date 
            ROWS BETWEEN 29 PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW
        ) AS ma_30_day_units,
        
        AVG(daily_revenue) OVER (
            ORDER BY sale_date 
            ROWS BETWEEN 29 PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW
        ) AS ma_30_day_revenue,
        
        -- 7-day median (robust short-term trend)
        MEDIAN(daily_units) OVER (
            ORDER BY sale_date 
            ROWS BETWEEN 6 PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW
        ) AS median_7_day_units,
        
        -- Cumulative average (overall trend from start)
        AVG(daily_units) OVER (
            ORDER BY sale_date 
            ROWS BETWEEN UNBOUNDED PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW
        ) AS cumulative_avg_units,
        
        -- Day-of-week averages (seasonality)
        AVG(daily_units) OVER (
            PARTITION BY day_of_week 
            ORDER BY sale_date 
            ROWS BETWEEN 3 PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW
        ) AS dow_ma_4_weeks
        
    FROM daily_aggregates
),
trend_analysis AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Trend direction detection
        CASE 
            WHEN ma_7_day_units > ma_30_day_units * 1.05 THEN 'SHORT_TERM_UPTREND'
            WHEN ma_7_day_units < ma_30_day_units * 0.95 THEN 'SHORT_TERM_DOWNTREND'
            ELSE 'STABLE_TREND'
        END AS trend_direction,
        
        -- Volatility: 7-day MA vs Median difference
        ABS(ma_7_day_units - median_7_day_units) / NULLIF(median_7_day_units, 0) * 100 AS ma_median_volatility_pct,
        
        -- Momentum: Rate of change in moving average
        ma_7_day_units - LAG(ma_7_day_units, 1) OVER (ORDER BY sale_date) AS ma_momentum,
        
        -- Seasonality detection
        daily_units - dow_ma_4_weeks AS seasonal_deviation,
        
        -- Inventory planning signal
        CASE 
            WHEN ma_7_day_units > ma_30_day_units * 1.1 AND daily_units > ma_7_day_units 
            THEN 'ACCELERATING_DEMAND_INCREASE_STOCK'
            WHEN ma_7_day_units < ma_30_day_units * 0.9 AND daily_units < ma_7_day_units 
            THEN 'DECELERATING_DEMAND_REDUCE_STOCK'
            WHEN ABS(seasonal_deviation) > ma_7_day_units * 0.2 
            THEN 'SEASONAL_ANOMALY_CHECK_CALENDAR'
            ELSE 'NORMAL_OPERATIONS'
        END AS inventory_signal
        
    FROM moving_calculations
)
SELECT 
    sale_date,
    day_name,
    
    -- Daily actuals
    daily_units AS actual_daily_sales,
    daily_revenue AS actual_daily_revenue,
    
    -- Moving averages comparison
    ROUND(ma_7_day_units, 1) AS short_term_avg_7_day,
    ROUND(ma_30_day_units, 1) AS long_term_avg_30_day,
    ROUND(median_7_day_units, 1) AS robust_median_7_day,
    ROUND(cumulative_avg_units, 1) AS cumulative_avg_to_date,
    
    -- Trend analysis
    trend_direction,
    ROUND(ma_momentum, 1) AS daily_momentum,
    ROUND(ma_median_volatility_pct, 1) AS volatility_indicator,
    
    -- Seasonality
    ROUND(dow_ma_4_weeks, 1) AS typical_for_this_day,
    ROUND(seasonal_deviation, 1) AS deviation_from_typical,
    
    -- Business signals
    inventory_signal AS operational_recommendation,
    
    -- Data quality: Is average meaningful today?
    CASE 
        WHEN sale_date < DATEADD(DAY, 30, (SELECT MIN(sale_date) FROM daily_aggregates))
        THEN 'INSUFFICIENT_DATA_FOR_30DAY_MA'
        WHEN ma_median_volatility_pct > 30 
        THEN 'HIGH_VOLATILITY_AVERAGES_UNRELIABLE'
        ELSE 'AVERAGES_RELIABLE'
    END AS data_quality_status
    
FROM trend_analysis

WHERE sale_date >= DATEADD(DAY, -60, CURRENT_DATE()) -- Last 60 days for display

ORDER BY sale_date DESC;
```

**Workflow Explanation**:

1. **7-Day Moving Average** - `AVG() OVER (ROWS 6 PRECEDING)` smooths daily noise for short-term trends
2. **30-Day Moving Average** - Longer window for baseline trend detection (inventory planning)
3. **Median Moving Average** - Robust alternative when daily outliers occur (promotions, holidays)
4. **Cumulative Average** - Overall trend from beginning of dataset (business growth trajectory)
5. **Day-of-Week Average** - Adjusts for Monday/Friday spikes (seasonal inventory planning)

    SELECT 
        payer_id,

        AVG(business_hours_to_decision) AS arithmetic_mean_hours,
        AVG(authorization_cost) AS arithmetic_mean_cost,

        MEDIAN(calendar_days_to_decision) AS median_days,
        MEDIAN(business_hours_to_decision) AS median_hours,
        MEDIAN(authorization_cost) AS median_cost,

        EXP(AVG(LN(NULLIF(business_hours_to_decision, 0)))) AS geometric_mean_hours,

        -- HM = COUNT / SUM(1/x) - appropriate for average rates

        MIN(calendar_days_to_decision) AS min_days,
        MAX(calendar_days_to_decision) AS max_days,

        SUM(CASE WHEN required_medical_review THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS medical_review_count

),

    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Mean comparisons (detecting skew)
        ROUND(arithmetic_mean_days / NULLIF(median_days, 0), 2) AS mean_to_median_ratio,
        ROUND(geometric_mean_days / NULLIF(arithmetic_mean_days, 0), 2) AS gm_to_am_ratio,
        ROUND(harmonic_mean_days / NULLIF(arithmetic_mean_days, 0), 2) AS hm_to_am_ratio,
        
        -- Inequality: AM >= GM >= HM (mathematical property verification)
        CASE 
            WHEN arithmetic_mean_days >= geometric_mean_days 
                 AND geometric_mean_days >= harmonic_mean_days 
            THEN 'MEAN_INEQUALITY_HOLDS'
            ELSE 'CALCULATION_ERROR'
        END AS mean_order_validation,
        
        -- Coefficient of variation
        ROUND(stddev_days / NULLIF(arithmetic_mean_days, 0) * 100, 2) AS cv_pct,
        
        -- Skewness indicator
        CASE 
            WHEN arithmetic_mean_days > median_days * 2 THEN 'HIGH_RIGHT_SKEW'
            WHEN arithmetic_mean_days > median_days * 1.5 THEN 'MODERATE_RIGHT_SKEW'
            WHEN arithmetic_mean_days < median_days THEN 'LEFT_SKEW'
            ELSE 'APPROX_SYMMETRIC'
        END AS distribution_skewness,
        
        -- Appropriate mean selection
        CASE 
            WHEN mean_to_median_ratio > 2 
            THEN 'USE_GEOMETRIC_OR_MEDIAN_FOR_TAT_REPORTING'
            WHEN urgent_count > 0 AND urgent_count = total_auths 
            THEN 'USE_HARMONIC_FOR_URGENT_RATE_ANALYSIS'
            WHEN cv_pct > 100 
            THEN 'USE_MEDIAN_AND_PERCENTILES_NOT_MEAN'
            ELSE 'ARITHMETIC_MEAN_ACCEPTABLE'
        END AS recommended_average
        
    FROM duration_calculations
)
SELECT 
    payer_id,
    total_auths,
    
    -- All means comparison
    ROUND(arithmetic_mean_days, 2) AS arithmetic_mean_tat_days,
    ROUND(median_days, 2) AS median_tat_days,
    ROUND(geometric_mean_days, 2) AS geometric_mean_tat_days,
    ROUND(harmonic_mean_days, 2) AS harmonic_mean_tat_days,
    mode_days AS most_common_tat_days,

    mean_to_median_ratio AS outlier_distortion_factor,
    distribution_skewness AS data_distribution_type,

    ROUND(arithmetic_mean_cost, 2) AS avg_cost_per_auth,
    ROUND(total_processing_cost, 2) AS total_auth_costs,
    
    -- Service level metrics
    p75_days AS tat_for_75pct_auths,
    p95_days AS tat_for_95pct_auths,
    max_days AS worst_case_tat,
    
    -- Operational insights
    urgent_count AS urgent_auths,
    medical_review_count AS medically_reviewed_auths,
    
    -- Critical recommendation
    recommended_average AS correct_metric_for_sla,
    
    -- Silent failure warning
    CASE 
        WHEN arithmetic_mean_days > 10 AND median_days < 3 
        THEN 'WARNING_MEAN_REPORTS_10DAYS_MOST_PATIENTS_GET_1DAY'
        WHEN p95_days > arithmetic_mean_days * 3 
        THEN 'WARNING_5PCT_PATIENTS_EXPERIENCE_EXTREME_DELAYS'
        ELSE 'METRICS_REPRESENT_EXPERIENCE'
    END AS patient_experience_alert,
    
    -- Mathematical validation
    mean_order_validation
    
FROM mean_comparison

ORDER BY 
    CASE 
        WHEN distribution_skewness = 'HIGH_RIGHT_SKEW' THEN 1

**Scenario**: Calculate conditional averages for EDI transaction analysis with quality filters.

```sql

-- AVERAGES QUERY: Conditional and filtered averages
WITH quality_classification AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Quality scoring (like data validation before averaging)
        CASE 
            WHEN error_count = 0 AND warning_count = 0 THEN 'CLEAN'
            WHEN error_count <= 2 AND warning_count <= 3 THEN 'ACCEPTABLE'
            WHEN error_count <= 5 OR warning_count <= 10 THEN 'REVIEW_REQUIRED'
            ELSE 'REJECT_QUALITY'
        END AS quality_tier,
        
        -- Size classification
        CASE 
            WHEN transaction_size_kb < 50 THEN 'SMALL'
            WHEN transaction_size_kb < 500 THEN 'MEDIUM'
            ELSE 'LARGE'
        END AS size_tier,
        
        -- Processing efficiency ratio
        processing_time_seconds / NULLIF(line_count, 0) AS seconds_per_line
        
    FROM unified_edi_transactions
),
conditional_averages AS (
    SELECT 
        edi_type,
        
        -- Unconditional averages (raw, includes all data)
        AVG(transaction_size_kb) AS raw_avg_size_kb,
        AVG(processing_time_seconds) AS raw_avg_processing_sec,
        AVG(error_count) AS raw_avg_errors,
        
        -- Conditional averages: Clean transactions only (quality filter)
        AVG(CASE WHEN quality_tier = 'CLEAN' THEN transaction_size_kb END) AS clean_avg_size_kb,
        AVG(CASE WHEN quality_tier = 'CLEAN' THEN processing_time_seconds END) AS clean_avg_processing_sec,
        
        -- Conditional averages: Acknowledged only (business valid)
        AVG(CASE WHEN is_acknowledged THEN processing_time_seconds END) AS acknowledged_avg_processing,
        AVG(CASE WHEN is_acknowledged THEN acknowledgment_time_seconds END) AS avg_ack_time,
        
        -- Conditional averages: By size tier (stratified analysis)
        AVG(CASE WHEN size_tier = 'SMALL' THEN processing_time_seconds END) AS small_trans_avg_time,
        AVG(CASE WHEN size_tier = 'MEDIUM' THEN processing_time_seconds END) AS medium_trans_avg_time,
        AVG(CASE WHEN size_tier = 'LARGE' THEN processing_time_seconds END) AS large_trans_avg_time,

        SUM(processing_time_seconds * line_count) / SUM(line_count) AS complexity_weighted_avg_time,
        
        -- Trimmed averages: Exclude top/bottom 10% (robust to outliers)
        AVG(CASE 
            WHEN processing_time_seconds >= PERCENTILE_CONT(0.1) WITHIN GROUP (ORDER BY processing_time_seconds) OVER ()
             AND processing_time_seconds <= PERCENTILE_CONT(0.9) WITHIN GROUP (ORDER BY processing_time_seconds) OVER ()
            THEN processing_time_seconds 
        END) AS trimmed_10pct_avg_time,
        
        -- Winsorized averages: Cap extremes instead of removing
        AVG(CASE 
            WHEN processing_time_seconds > (SELECT PERCENTILE_CONT(0.95) FROM unified_edi_transactions WHERE edi_type = u.edi_type) 
            THEN (SELECT PERCENTILE_CONT(0.95) FROM unified_edi_transactions WHERE edi_type = u.edi_type)
            ELSE processing_time_seconds 
        END) AS winsorized_95pct_avg,
        
        -- Volume metrics
        COUNT(*) AS total_transactions,
        COUNT(CASE WHEN quality_tier = 'CLEAN' THEN 1 END) AS clean_transaction_count,
        COUNT(CASE WHEN is_acknowledged THEN 1 END) AS acknowledged_count
        
    FROM quality_classification u
    
    GROUP BY edi_type
),
average_quality_analysis AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Quality impact: Clean vs Raw difference
        ROUND((raw_avg_processing_sec - clean_avg_processing_sec) / 
              NULLIF(raw_avg_processing_sec, 0) * 100, 2) AS quality_penalty_pct,
        
        -- Acknowledgment rate
        ROUND(acknowledged_count * 100.0 / total_transactions, 2) AS acknowledgment_rate,
        
        -- Outlier impact: Raw vs Trimmed
        ROUND((raw_avg_processing_sec - trimmed_10pct_avg_time) / 
              NULLIF(trimmed_10pct_avg_time, 0) * 100, 2) AS outlier_inflation_pct,
        
        -- Data sufficiency for averages
        CASE 
            WHEN clean_transaction_count < 10 THEN 'INSUFFICIENT_CLEAN_DATA_FOR_AVG'
            WHEN acknowledged_count < 10 THEN 'INSUFFICIENT_ACKNOWLEDGED_DATA'
            ELSE 'SUFFICIENT_SAMPLE_SIZE'
        END AS sample_size_adequacy,
        
        -- Recommended reporting average
        CASE 
            WHEN quality_penalty_pct > 20 THEN 'USE_CLEAN_AVG_QUALITY_ISSUES_PRESENT'
            WHEN outlier_inflation_pct > 30 THEN 'USE_TRIMMED_OR_MEDIAN_AVG'
            WHEN acknowledgment_rate < 80 THEN 'USE_ACKNOWLEDGED_ONLY_AVG'
            ELSE 'RAW_AVG_ACCEPTABLE'
        END AS recommended_baseline_metric
        
    FROM conditional_averages
)
SELECT 
    edi_type,
    total_transactions,
    
    -- Average comparison matrix
    ROUND(raw_avg_processing_sec, 2) AS unfiltered_avg_seconds,
    ROUND(clean_avg_processing_sec, 2) AS clean_only_avg_seconds,
    ROUND(acknowledged_avg_processing, 2) AS business_valid_avg_seconds,
    ROUND(trimmed_10pct_avg_time, 2) AS robust_trimmed_avg_seconds,
    ROUND(winsorized_95pct_avg, 2) AS capped_avg_seconds,
    ROUND(complexity_weighted_avg_time, 2) AS complexity_weighted_avg,
    
    -- Size-stratified averages
    ROUND(small_trans_avg_time, 2) AS small_trans_avg,
    ROUND(medium_trans_avg_time, 2) AS medium_trans_avg,
    ROUND(large_trans_avg_time, 2) AS large_trans_avg,
    
    -- Quality metrics
    clean_transaction_count AS clean_trans_count,
    quality_penalty_pct AS error_impact_on_avg,
    acknowledgment_rate AS pct_acknowledged,
    outlier_inflation_pct AS extreme_value_distortion,
    
    -- Data reliability
    sample_size_adequacy,
    recommended_baseline_metric AS use_this_average_for_kpi,
    
    -- Business insight
    CASE 
        WHEN quality_penalty_pct > 50 
        THEN 'CRITICAL_QUALITY_ISSUES_INFLATING_AVERAGES'
        WHEN outlier_inflation_pct > 40 
        THEN 'EXTREME_OUTLIERS_DISTORTING_PERFORMANCE_VIEW'
        WHEN acknowledgment_rate < 50 
        THEN 'HIGH_FAILURE_RATE_AVERAGES_MISLEADING'
        ELSE 'AVERAGES_REPRESENT_TRUE_PERFORMANCE'
    END AS data_interpretation_warning
    
FROM average_quality_analysis

ORDER BY 
    CASE edi_type 
        WHEN '837' THEN 1 
        WHEN '835' THEN 2 
        WHEN '852' THEN 3 
        WHEN '840' THEN 4 
    END;
```

1. **Raw Average** - Unfiltered `AVG()` (includes error-ridden transactions)
2. **Clean Average** - `AVG(CASE WHEN quality_tier = 'CLEAN')` (quality-filtered)
3. **Acknowledged Average** - Business-valid transactions only (operational reality)
4. **Trimmed Average** - Excludes top/bottom 10% (robust to outliers without full removal)
5. **Winsorized Average** - Caps extremes at 95th percentile (reduces outlier impact while retaining data)
6. **Stratified Averages** - Separate averages by transaction size (small/medium/large)

## Key Averages Concepts Summary

| Average Type | Formula | Best For | Pharma Use Case | Outlier Sensitivity |
|-------------|---------|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Arithmetic Mean** | SUM(x)/COUNT | Symmetric distributions | Budget planning, resource allocation | High |
| **Median** | Middle value | Skewed distributions | Patient wait times, drug prices | None |
| **Mode** | Most frequent | Categorical data | Most common dosage, popular drugs | None |
| **Geometric Mean** | EXP(AVG(LN(x))) | Growth rates, ratios | Compound annual growth, adherence rates | Moderate |
| **Harmonic Mean** | COUNT/SUM(1/x) | Rates, ratios | Average speed, processing rates | High (for small values) |
| **Weighted Mean** | SUM(x*w)/SUM(w) | Heterogeneous data | Revenue-weighted prices, volume metrics | Depends on weights |
| **Trimmed Mean** | Mean of middle X% | Contaminated data | Clean performance metrics | Low |
| **Moving Average** | Mean of window | Time series trends | Inventory planning, sales forecasting | Moderate |

**Critical Warnings for Pharma Data**:

1. **Mean-Median Divergence** - When ratio > 2, report median to avoid misleading stakeholders
2. **Sample Size** - Averages with n<30 are unreliable; require confidence intervals
3. **Right Skew** - Common in healthcare (many short visits, few long ones) - use geometric mean or median
4. **Zero Handling** - Harmonic and geometric means fail with zeros - add small constant or filter
5. **Business Context** - Urgent care averages should use harmonic mean (rate-focused), not arithmetic

**Remember**: Just like the $50K salary example with executives, a $1,200 specialty drug can make a $0.75 generic average meaningless. Always check distribution shape before selecting your average!

> [!Tip]
> To get a better sense of the data distribution, try calculating and comparing different types of averages, such as mean, median, and mode.

## Key Points
- The mean average is sensitive to extreme values and can be skewed by outliers.
- The median average is more robust to outliers and provides a better representation of the data's central tendency when the distribution is skewed.
- The mode average represents the most frequently occurring value in the dataset.
- Averages can be used to compare datasets and identify trends over time.

> [!important]
> The data must be measured at least on an interval or ratio scale to calculate meaningful averages.

## Mental Model
1. **Data Collection**: Gather a dataset of values that are measured on an interval or ratio scale.
2. **Data Analysis**: Calculate the average using the appropriate formula (e.g., mean, median, mode).
3. **Data Interpretation**: Compare the average to other datasets or benchmarks to identify trends, patterns, and insights.

## Observe
- A common mistake people make is assuming that the mean average always represents the typical value in a dataset.
- A silent failure mode is when averages are calculated without considering the data distribution, leading to misleading conclusions.
- A misleading assumption is that averages always provide a complete picture of the data, when in fact they can mask important variations and nuances.

> [!Important]
> For example, a company's average employee salary might be $50,000, but this value could be skewed by a few highly paid executives, hiding the fact that most employees earn significantly less.
