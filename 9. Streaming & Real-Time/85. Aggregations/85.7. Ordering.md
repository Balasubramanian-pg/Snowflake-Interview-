# 85.7. Ordering

> [!Note]
> Effective ordering is crucial for streamlining processes, reducing costs, and enhancing customer satisfaction, and it can be achieved by implementing a well-structured approach to managing orders.

## What is Ordering in Pharma Supply Chain?

In pharmaceutical operations, **ordering** is the systematic process of replenishing inventory based on demand signals, stock levels, and supply constraints. Unlike the mathematical concept of ordering (sorting), this refers to **purchase ordering workflows**—critical for:
- **Inventory optimization** (preventing stockouts of life-saving medications)
- **Demand forecasting** (using claims data to predict future needs)
- **Supplier management** (EDI 840/852 transactions)
- **Cold chain and expiry management** (FIFO for perishables)

## Use Case 1: Reorder Point Calculations (ROP)

**Scenario**: Calculate when to place new orders using EDI 852 inventory and 840 quote data with dynamic reorder points.

```sql
-- Create inventory position and consumption data
CREATE OR REPLACE TABLE inventory_ordering_base (
    ndc_code VARCHAR(11),
    warehouse_id VARCHAR(20),
    current_on_hand INTEGER,
    current_reserved INTEGER,
    in_transit_quantity INTEGER,
    on_order_quantity INTEGER,
    
    -- Consumption history (from retail sales/claims)
    avg_daily_demand DECIMAL(10,2),
    max_daily_demand DECIMAL(10,2),
    days_of_history INTEGER,
    
    -- Supplier data (from EDI 840)
    primary_supplier_id VARCHAR(20),
    supplier_lead_time_days INTEGER,
    supplier_reliability_score DECIMAL(5,2), -- 0-100
    
    -- Product characteristics
    unit_cost DECIMAL(10,2),
    is_critical_drug BOOLEAN,
    shelf_life_days INTEGER,
    storage_requirements VARCHAR(20) -- 'ambient', 'refrigerated', 'frozen'
);

-- Insert sample inventory data
INSERT INTO inventory_ordering_base VALUES
('00093-1058-01', 'WH_EAST', 1500, 300, 2000, 0, 45.5, 78.0, 90, 'SUPPLIER_A', 7, 95.5, 0.32, FALSE, 1095, 'ambient'),
('00093-2748-01', 'WH_EAST', 800, 150, 0, 1000, 120.0, 200.0, 90, 'SUPPLIER_A', 5, 98.0, 0.75, TRUE, 1095, 'ambient'),
('61755-001-01', 'WH_EAST', 50, 10, 0, 0, 5.2, 12.0, 30, 'SUPPLIER_B', 14, 88.0, 850.00, TRUE, 730, 'ambient'),
('00078-0429-61', 'WH_WEST', 25, 5, 100, 0, 2.1, 5.0, 60, 'SUPPLIER_C', 21, 92.0, 1200.00, TRUE, 545, 'refrigerated'),
('00555-0808-02', 'WH_WEST', 2500, 400, 5000, 2000, 85.0, 150.0, 90, 'SUPPLIER_A', 3, 99.0, 1.45, FALSE, 1095, 'ambient');

-- ORDERING QUERY: Reorder point and order quantity calculations
WITH inventory_position AS (
    -- Calculate effective inventory position (on hand + in transit - reserved)
    SELECT 
        ndc_code,
        warehouse_id,
        current_on_hand,
        current_reserved,
        in_transit_quantity,
        on_order_quantity,
        
        -- Effective available inventory (what we can actually use)
        current_on_hand - current_reserved AS available_inventory,
        
        -- Total pipeline inventory (physical + ordered + transit)
        current_on_hand + in_transit_quantity + on_order_quantity AS total_pipeline,
        
        -- Net position (available + in transit - on order)
        current_on_hand - current_reserved + in_transit_quantity AS net_available_position,
        
        avg_daily_demand,
        max_daily_demand,
        supplier_lead_time_days,
        supplier_reliability_score,
        unit_cost,
        is_critical_drug,
        shelf_life_days
        
    FROM inventory_ordering_base
),
reorder_calculations AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Safety stock calculation (higher for critical drugs and unreliable suppliers)
        -- Formula: Safety Stock = (Max Daily Demand - Avg Daily Demand) × Lead Time × Reliability Factor
        CASE 
            WHEN is_critical_drug THEN 
                (max_daily_demand - avg_daily_demand) * supplier_lead_time_days * (1 + (100 - supplier_reliability_score)/100)
            ELSE 
                (max_daily_demand - avg_daily_demand) * supplier_lead_time_days * 0.5
        END AS safety_stock_units,
        
        -- Reorder Point (ROP) = (Avg Demand × Lead Time) + Safety Stock
        (avg_daily_demand * supplier_lead_time_days) + 
            CASE 
                WHEN is_critical_drug THEN 
                    (max_daily_demand - avg_daily_demand) * supplier_lead_time_days * (1 + (100 - supplier_reliability_score)/100)
                ELSE 
                    (max_daily_demand - avg_daily_demand) * supplier_lead_time_days * 0.5
            END AS reorder_point_units,
        
        -- Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) approximation
        -- EOQ = sqrt((2 × Demand × Order Cost) / Holding Cost)
        -- Simplified: Order 30 days of supply for standard, 14 for critical/high cost
        CASE 
            WHEN is_critical_drug AND unit_cost > 100 THEN avg_daily_demand * 14  -- 2 weeks for expensive critical
            WHEN unit_cost > 500 THEN avg_daily_demand * 7  -- 1 week for very expensive
            ELSE avg_daily_demand * 30  -- 1 month for standard
        END AS suggested_order_quantity,
        
        -- Days of supply remaining
        CASE 
            WHEN avg_daily_demand > 0 THEN (current_on_hand - current_reserved) / avg_daily_demand
            ELSE 999
        END AS days_of_supply_remaining
        
    FROM inventory_position
),
order_decisions AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Order trigger logic (the "ordering threshold")
        CASE 
            WHEN net_available_position <= reorder_point_units THEN 'PLACE_ORDER_NOW'
            WHEN net_available_position <= reorder_point_units * 1.2 THEN 'PLAN_ORDER_SOON'
            WHEN days_of_supply_remaining <= supplier_lead_time_days * 1.5 THEN 'MONITOR_CLOSELY'
            ELSE 'ADEQUATE_STOCK'
        END AS order_status,
        
        -- Urgency classification
        CASE 
            WHEN days_of_supply_remaining <= supplier_lead_time_days AND is_critical_drug 
            THEN 'CRITICAL_STOCKOUT_RISK'
            WHEN days_of_supply_remaining <= supplier_lead_time_days * 0.5 
            THEN 'HIGH_URGENCY'
            WHEN days_of_supply_remaining <= supplier_lead_time_days 
            THEN 'MEDIUM_URGENCY'
            ELSE 'ROUTINE_REPLENISHMENT'
        END AS urgency_level,
        
        -- Order value calculation
        suggested_order_quantity * unit_cost AS estimated_order_value
        
    FROM reorder_calculations
)
SELECT 
    warehouse_id,
    ndc_code,
    
    -- Current position
    current_on_hand AS physical_on_hand,
    current_reserved AS committed_to_orders,
    available_inventory AS available_to_sell,
    in_transit_quantity AS incoming_stock,
    on_order_quantity AS pending_orders,
    net_available_position AS net_position,
    
    -- Demand metrics
    ROUND(avg_daily_demand, 1) AS avg_daily_usage,
    ROUND(max_daily_demand, 1) AS peak_daily_usage,
    ROUND(days_of_supply_remaining, 1) AS days_supply_left,
    
    -- Ordering parameters
    supplier_lead_time_days AS lead_time,
    ROUND(safety_stock_units, 0) AS safety_stock_level,
    ROUND(reorder_point_units, 0) AS reorder_point,
    ROUND(suggested_order_quantity, 0) AS suggested_order_qty,
    
    -- Decision
    order_status AS action_required,
    urgency_level AS priority,
    
    -- Financials
    unit_cost,
    ROUND(estimated_order_value, 2) AS order_value,
    
    -- Silent failure detection (appears adequate but isn't)
    CASE 
        WHEN current_on_hand > reorder_point_units * 2 AND available_inventory < reorder_point_units 
        THEN 'ALERT_HIGH_RESERVED_STOCK_LOCKED'
        WHEN in_transit_quantity = 0 AND on_order_quantity = 0 AND days_of_supply_remaining <= supplier_lead_time_days * 2
        THEN 'ALERT_NO_PIPELINE_STOCK'
        WHEN shelf_life_days < 90 AND current_on_hand > avg_daily_demand * 60
        THEN 'ALERT_EXPIRY_RISK_OVERSTOCK'
        ELSE 'INVENTORY_HEALTHY'
    END AS stock_status_warning
    
FROM order_decisions

ORDER BY 
    CASE urgency_level 
        WHEN 'CRITICAL_STOCKOUT_RISK' THEN 1
        WHEN 'HIGH_URGENCY' THEN 2
        WHEN 'MEDIUM_URGENCY' THEN 3
        ELSE 4
    END,
    estimated_order_value DESC;
```

**Workflow Explanation**:

1. **Inventory Position Calculation** - Computes net available (on hand - reserved + in transit) to determine true stock position
2. **Safety Stock Formula** - Adjusts buffer inventory based on demand variability, lead time, and supplier reliability (higher for critical drugs)
3. **Reorder Point Logic** - Triggers orders when net position hits threshold (demand during lead time + safety stock)
4. **EOQ Suggestion** - Recommends order quantities based on drug criticality and cost (JIT for expensive drugs)
5. **Silent Failure Detection** - Identifies when physical stock looks adequate but reserved stock creates risk

## Use Case 2: FIFO (First-In-First-Out) Inventory Management

**Scenario**: Manage inventory ordering and dispensing using FIFO to minimize expiration waste.

```sql
-- Create lot-level inventory with expiration dates
CREATE OR REPLACE TABLE lot_inventory (
    inventory_id VARCHAR(30),
    ndc_code VARCHAR(11),
    lot_number VARCHAR(20),
    expiration_date DATE,
    quantity_received INTEGER,
    quantity_remaining INTEGER,
    date_received DATE,
    warehouse_id VARCHAR(20),
    bin_location VARCHAR(20),
    unit_cost DECIMAL(10,4),
    is_quarantine BOOLEAN
);

-- Insert lot data with varying expiration dates (FIFO scenario)
INSERT INTO lot_inventory VALUES
('INV_001', '00093-1058-01', 'LOT_A_2023', '2025-06-01', 5000, 1200, '2023-06-01', 'WH_EAST', 'A-01', 0.2800, FALSE),
('INV_002', '00093-1058-01', 'LOT_B_2023', '2025-09-01', 5000, 3800, '2023-09-01', 'WH_EAST', 'A-02', 0.2900, FALSE),
('INV_003', '00093-1058-01', 'LOT_C_2024', '2026-03-01', 3000, 3000, '2024-03-01', 'WH_EAST', 'A-03', 0.3200, FALSE),
('INV_004', '00078-0429-61', 'LOT_H_2024', '2025-01-15', 500, 450, '2024-01-15', 'WH_WEST', 'R-01', 1100.0000, FALSE),
('INV_005', '00078-0429-61', 'LOT_H_2024B', '2025-04-20', 300, 300, '2024-02-20', 'WH_WEST', 'R-02', 1150.0000, FALSE),
('INV_006', '61755-001-01', 'LOT_R_OLD', '2024-12-01', 1000, 200, '2023-12-01', 'WH_EAST', 'S-01', 800.0000, FALSE),
('INV_007', '61755-001-01', 'LOT_R_NEW', '2025-06-01', 800, 800, '2024-03-01', 'WH_EAST', 'S-02', 820.0000, FALSE);

-- ORDERING QUERY: FIFO sequencing and expiration management
WITH lot_ranking AS (
    -- Rank lots by expiration date (FIFO) and receipt date (FEFO - First Expired First Out)
    SELECT 
        ndc_code,
        warehouse_id,
        inventory_id,
        lot_number,
        expiration_date,
        quantity_remaining,
        unit_cost,
        date_received,
        is_quarantine,
        
        -- Days until expiration
        DATEDIFF(DAY, CURRENT_DATE(), expiration_date) AS days_until_expiry,
        
        -- FIFO ranking (oldest expiration first)
        ROW_NUMBER() OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code, warehouse_id 
                          ORDER BY expiration_date ASC, date_received ASC) AS fifo_rank,
        
        -- FEFO ranking (most urgent expiry first - same as FIFO but explicit)
        RANK() OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code, warehouse_id 
                    ORDER BY expiration_date ASC) AS fefo_rank,
        
        -- Cumulative quantity (running total for FIFO picking)
        SUM(quantity_remaining) OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code, warehouse_id 
                                     ORDER BY expiration_date ASC, date_received ASC 
                                     ROWS BETWEEN UNBOUNDED PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW) AS cumulative_quantity,
        
        -- Total inventory for this NDC/warehouse
        SUM(quantity_remaining) OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code, warehouse_id) AS total_inventory
        
    FROM lot_inventory
    WHERE is_quarantine = FALSE  -- Exclude quarantined lots from available inventory
),
fifo_analysis AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Determine which lots to pick from first (FIFO)
        CASE 
            WHEN fifo_rank = 1 THEN 'PICK_FIRST'
            WHEN cumulative_quantity <= total_inventory * 0.5 THEN 'PICK_EARLY'
            ELSE 'PICK_LAST'
        END AS picking_priority,
        
        -- Expiration risk classification
        CASE 
            WHEN days_until_expiry <= 30 THEN 'CRITICAL_EXPIRE_30_DAYS'
            WHEN days_until_expiry <= 90 THEN 'WARNING_EXPIRE_90_DAYS'
            WHEN days_until_expiry <= 180 THEN 'MONITOR_EXPIRE_6_MONTHS'
            ELSE 'HEALTHY_SHELF_LIFE'
        END AS expiry_status,
        
        -- Financial impact of expiration (at risk value)
        quantity_remaining * unit_cost AS inventory_value_at_risk,
        
        -- Recommended disposal/transfer if nearing expiry
        CASE 
            WHEN days_until_expiry <= 60 AND quantity_remaining > 0 THEN 'URGENT_DISPENSE_OR_TRANSFER'
            WHEN days_until_expiry <= 120 AND fifo_rank > 1 THEN 'CONSIDER_TRANSFER_TO_HIGHER_VOLUME_SITE'
            ELSE 'NORMAL_ROTATION'
        END AS inventory_action
        
    FROM lot_ranking
),
ordering_recommendations AS (
    SELECT 
        ndc_code,
        warehouse_id,
        
        -- Total position
        MAX(total_inventory) AS total_units,
        SUM(CASE WHEN days_until_expiry <= 90 THEN quantity_remaining ELSE 0 END) AS units_expiring_90_days,
        
        -- FIFO compliance metrics
        SUM(CASE WHEN fifo_rank = 1 THEN quantity_remaining ELSE 0 END) AS oldest_lot_quantity,
        MIN(CASE WHEN quantity_remaining > 0 THEN expiration_date END) AS earliest_expiry_date,
        
        -- Financial risk
        SUM(inventory_value_at_risk) AS total_inventory_value,
        SUM(CASE WHEN days_until_expiry <= 90 THEN inventory_value_at_risk ELSE 0 END) AS value_at_risk_90_days,
        
        -- Ordering constraint: Don't order if oldest lot is > X days supply
        CASE 
            WHEN MAX(CASE WHEN fifo_rank = 1 THEN days_until_expiry END) > 180 
            THEN 'DEFER_ORDER_OLD_STOCK_SUFFICIENT'
            WHEN SUM(CASE WHEN days_until_expiry <= 90 THEN quantity_remaining ELSE 0 END) > 
                 MAX(total_inventory) * 0.3
            THEN 'CAUTION_30PCT_STOCK_NEAR_EXPIRY'
            ELSE 'FIFO_COMPLIANT_OK_TO_ORDER'
        END AS ordering_constraint
        
    FROM fifo_analysis
    
    GROUP BY ndc_code, warehouse_id
)
SELECT 
    f.ndc_code,
    f.warehouse_id,
    f.lot_number,
    f.expiration_date,
    f.quantity_remaining AS units_in_lot,
    f.days_until_expiry,
    f.fifo_rank AS consumption_sequence,
    f.picking_priority AS pick_order,
    f.expiry_status AS expiration_alert,
    
    -- Financial view
    ROUND(f.unit_cost, 4) AS unit_cost,
    ROUND(f.inventory_value_at_risk, 2) AS lot_value,
    
    -- Action items
    f.inventory_action AS recommended_action,
    
    -- Aggregate constraints
    o.ordering_constraint AS new_order_approval_status,
    ROUND(o.value_at_risk_90_days, 2) AS near_term_write_off_risk,
    
    -- Silent failure: Inventory looks sufficient but is expiring
    CASE 
        WHEN o.total_units > 1000 AND o.units_expiring_90_days > o.total_units * 0.5
        THEN 'SILENT_FAILURE_50PCT_STOCK_EXPIRING_SOON'
        WHEN f.fifo_rank > 1 AND f.days_until_expiry < 30 AND f.quantity_remaining > 0
        THEN 'VIOLATION_NEWER_LOT_CONSUMED_BEFORE_OLDER'
        ELSE 'FIFO_COMPLIANT'
    END AS compliance_status
    
FROM fifo_analysis f
JOIN ordering_recommendations o 
    ON f.ndc_code = o.ndc_code AND f.warehouse_id = o.warehouse_id

ORDER BY f.ndc_code, f.warehouse_id, f.fifo_rank;
```

**Workflow Explanation**:

1. **Lot Ranking** - `ROW_NUMBER()` orders lots by expiration date (oldest first = FIFO sequence)
2. **Cumulative Sum** - Running total shows which lots cover demand (first expired, first out)
3. **Expiry Risk Tiers** - Classifies lots by days until expiration (30/90/180 day buckets)
4. **Picking Priority** - Flags oldest lots as "PICK_FIRST" for warehouse operations
5. **Silent Failure Detection** - Identifies when total inventory looks adequate but 50% is near expiry

## Use Case 3: Demand Forecasting from Claims Data (837)

**Scenario**: Use historical EDI 837 claims to forecast future ordering needs using moving averages and seasonality.

```sql
-- Create historical demand from claims
CREATE OR REPLACE TABLE historical_demand (
    demand_date DATE,
    ndc_code VARCHAR(11),
    pharmacy_npi VARCHAR(10),
    claim_count INTEGER,
    units_dispensed INTEGER,
    unique_patients INTEGER,
    is_weekend BOOLEAN,
    is_holiday BOOLEAN,
    season_quarter INTEGER,
    day_of_week INTEGER
);

-- Generate 2 years of historical demand data
INSERT INTO historical_demand 
WITH date_spine AS (
    SELECT DATEADD(DAY, -seq, CURRENT_DATE()) AS demand_date
    FROM TABLE(GENERATOR(ROWCOUNT => 730)) AS gen(seq) -- 2 years
),
products AS (
    SELECT '00093-1058-01' AS ndc, 50 AS base_demand UNION ALL
    SELECT '00093-2748-01', 120 UNION ALL
    SELECT '00555-0808-02', 85
)
SELECT 
    ds.demand_date,
    p.ndc AS ndc_code,
    'PHARM001' AS pharmacy_npi,
    -- Simulate demand with trend, seasonality, and noise
    FLOOR(p.base_demand * 0.8 + 
          (SIN(DAYOFYEAR(ds.demand_date) * 0.1) * p.base_demand * 0.2) + -- Seasonality
          (DATEDIFF(DAY, ds.demand_date, CURRENT_DATE()) * 0.01) + -- Trend growth
          UNIFORM(-10, 10, RANDOM())) AS claim_count,
    FLOOR((p.base_demand * 0.8 + 
          (SIN(DAYOFYEAR(ds.demand_date) * 0.1) * p.base_demand * 0.2) + 
          (DATEDIFF(DAY, ds.demand_date, CURRENT_DATE()) * 0.01) + 
          UNIFORM(-10, 10, RANDOM())) * 1.5) AS units_dispensed,
    FLOOR((p.base_demand * 0.8) * 0.7) AS unique_patients,
    DAYOFWEEK(ds.demand_date) IN (0, 6) AS is_weekend,
    FALSE AS is_holiday, -- Simplified
    QUARTER(ds.demand_date) AS season_quarter,
    DAYOFWEEK(ds.demand_date) AS day_of_week
FROM date_spine ds
CROSS JOIN products p;

-- ORDERING QUERY: Demand forecasting for purchase orders
WITH daily_aggregation AS (
    -- Aggregate demand by day across all pharmacies
    SELECT 
        demand_date,
        ndc_code,
        SUM(units_dispensed) AS daily_units,
        SUM(claim_count) AS daily_claims,
        AVG(unique_patients) AS avg_daily_patients,
        season_quarter,
        day_of_week
    FROM historical_demand
    GROUP BY demand_date, ndc_code, season_quarter, day_of_week
),
forecasting_metrics AS (
    SELECT 
        ndc_code,
        demand_date,
        daily_units,
        
        -- Moving averages for trend
        AVG(daily_units) OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code ORDER BY demand_date ROWS BETWEEN 6 PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW) AS ma_7_day,
        AVG(daily_units) OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code ORDER BY demand_date ROWS BETWEEN 29 PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW) AS ma_30_day,
        AVG(daily_units) OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code ORDER BY demand_date ROWS BETWEEN 89 PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW) AS ma_90_day,
        
        -- Seasonal averages (same day of week)
        AVG(daily_units) OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code, day_of_week ORDER BY demand_date ROWS BETWEEN 12 PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW) AS dow_average,
        
        -- Exponential smoothing approximation (recent weighted more heavily)
        (daily_units * 0.3) + 
            (LAG(daily_units, 1) OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code ORDER BY demand_date) * 0.25) +
            (LAG(daily_units, 2) OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code ORDER BY demand_date) * 0.2) +
            (LAG(daily_units, 3) OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code ORDER BY demand_date) * 0.15) +
            (LAG(daily_units, 4) OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code ORDER BY demand_date) * 0.1) AS exp_smooth_forecast,
        
        -- Volatility (standard deviation)
        STDDEV(daily_units) OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code ORDER BY demand_date ROWS BETWEEN 29 PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW) AS demand_volatility,
        
        -- Growth trend (slope)
        (daily_units - LAG(daily_units, 30) OVER (PARTITION BY ndc_code ORDER BY demand_date)) / 30.0 AS daily_growth_slope
        
    FROM daily_aggregation
),
current_forecast AS (
    SELECT 
        ndc_code,
        -- Use most recent 30-day MA as baseline forecast
        AVG(ma_30_day) AS forecasted_daily_demand,
        AVG(demand_volatility) AS forecast_volatility,
        AVG(daily_growth_slope) AS trend_slope,
        
        -- Seasonal factor (Q1 average vs overall average)
        AVG(CASE WHEN season_quarter = QUARTER(CURRENT_DATE()) THEN daily_units END) / 
            NULLIF(AVG(daily_units), 0) AS seasonal_factor,
            
        -- Max observed for safety stock calculation
        MAX(daily_units) AS max_observed_daily_demand
        
    FROM forecasting_metrics
    WHERE demand_date >= DATEADD(DAY, -90, CURRENT_DATE()) -- Last 90 days only
    
    GROUP BY ndc_code
),
order_planning AS (
    SELECT 
        f.ndc_code,
        
        -- Forecast components
        ROUND(f.forecasted_daily_demand, 1) AS predicted_daily_usage,
        ROUND(f.forecast_volatility, 1) AS demand_variability,
        ROUND(f.trend_slope, 2) AS daily_trend,
        ROUND(f.seasonal_factor, 2) AS seasonal_multiplier,
        f.max_observed_daily_demand AS peak_historical_demand,
        
        -- Adjusted forecast (trend + seasonal)
        ROUND(f.forecasted_daily_demand * 
              CASE 
                  WHEN f.trend_slope > 0 THEN (1 + (f.trend_slope * 30 / NULLIF(f.forecasted_daily_demand, 0)))
                  ELSE 1
              END * 
              COALESCE(f.seasonal_factor, 1), 1) AS adjusted_forecast_30_days,
        
        -- Order calculation for next period
        ROUND(f.forecasted_daily_demand * 30 * COALESCE(f.seasonal_factor, 1)) AS suggested_monthly_order,
        
        -- Safety stock based on volatility
        ROUND(f.forecast_volatility * 7) AS safety_stock_for_1_week_volatility
        
    FROM current_forecast f
)
SELECT 
    op.ndc_code,
    op.predicted_daily_usage,
    op.demand_variability,
    op.daily_trend AS trend_direction,
    op.seasonal_multiplier,
    op.adjusted_forecast_30_days AS next_month_predicted_needs,
    op.suggested_monthly_order AS base_order_quantity,
    op.safety_stock_for_1_week_volatility AS volatility_buffer,
    
    -- Ordering recommendation
    CASE 
        WHEN op.daily_trend > op.predicted_daily_usage * 0.05 THEN 'INCREASE_ORDER_GROWING_DEMAND'
        WHEN op.daily_trend < -op.predicted_daily_usage * 0.05 THEN 'DECREASE_ORDER_DECLINING_DEMAND'
        WHEN op.demand_variability > op.predicted_daily_usage * 0.5 THEN 'HIGH_VARIABILITY_INCREASE_SAFETY_STOCK'
        ELSE 'STABLE_DEMAND_MAINTAIN_CURRENT_LEVELS'
    END AS ordering_strategy,
    
    -- Warning for common mistake (ordering based on last week only)
    CASE 
        WHEN op.predicted_daily_usage < op.peak_historical_demand * 0.5 
        THEN 'WARNING_RECENT_LOW_DEMAY_BE_ANOMALY_CHECK_90_DAY_TREND'
        WHEN op.seasonal_multiplier > 1.3 
        THEN 'WARNING_PEAK_SEASON_ADJUST_ORDER_UP'
        WHEN op.seasonal_multiplier < 0.7 
        THEN 'WARNING_SLOW_SEASON_AVOID_OVERSTOCK'
    END AS demand_alert
    
FROM order_planning op

ORDER BY op.adjusted_forecast_30_days DESC;
```

**Workflow Explanation**:

1. **Time Series Aggregation** - Aggregates daily demand from claims (837 dispense events)
2. **Moving Averages** - Calculates 7/30/90-day MAs to smooth out noise (trend identification)
3. **Seasonal Adjustment** - Compares current quarter vs. average (seasonal factor for ordering)
4. **Exponential Smoothing** - Weights recent demand higher (responsive to trend changes)
5. **Trend Slope** - Calculates growth/decline rate (avoid ordering based on stale data)

## Use Case 4: Lead Time Variance and Safety Stock (EDI 840/852)

**Scenario**: Account for supplier lead time variability when placing EDI 840 orders to prevent stockouts.

```sql
-- Create purchase order history with actual vs. promised lead times
CREATE OR REPLACE TABLE purchase_order_history (
    po_number VARCHAR(30),
    ndc_code VARCHAR(11),
    supplier_id VARCHAR(20),
    order_date DATE,
    promised_delivery_date DATE,
    actual_delivery_date DATE,
    quantity_ordered INTEGER,
    quantity_received INTEGER,
    unit_price DECIMAL(10,2),
    order_status VARCHAR(20) -- 'on_time', 'late', 'early', 'partial', 'cancelled'

('PO_010', '00078-0429-61', 'SUPPLIER_C', '2024-02-01', '2024-02-22', '2024-02-20', 150, 150, 1200.00, 'early');

-- ORDERING QUERY: Lead time analysis for accurate ordering
WITH lead_time_calculation AS (
    SELECT 
        ndc_code,
        supplier_id,
        po_number,
        order_date,
        promised_delivery_date,
        actual_delivery_date,
        quantity_ordered,
        quantity_received,
        
        -- Actual lead time (order to delivery)
        DATEDIFF(DAY, order_date, actual_delivery_date) AS actual_lead_time_days,
        
        -- Promised lead time
        DATEDIFF(DAY, order_date, promised_delivery_date) AS promised_lead_time_days,
        
        -- Variance (negative = early, positive = late)
        DATEDIFF(DAY, promised_delivery_date, actual_delivery_date) AS delivery_variance_days,
        
        -- Fill rate
        quantity_received * 1.0 / NULLIF(quantity_ordered, 0) AS fill_rate
        
    FROM purchase_order_history
    WHERE order_status != 'cancelled'
),
supplier_performance AS (
    SELECT 
        ndc_code,
        supplier_id,
        
        -- Lead time statistics
        AVG(actual_lead_time_days) AS avg_actual_lead_time,
        STDDEV(actual_lead_time_days) AS lead_time_stddev,
        MIN(actual_lead_time_days) AS min_lead_time,
        MAX(actual_lead_time_days) AS max_lead_time,
        
        -- Reliability metrics
        AVG(delivery_variance_days) AS avg_lateness_days,
        COUNT(CASE WHEN delivery_variance_days <= 0 THEN 1 END) * 100.0 / COUNT(*) AS on_time_delivery_pct,
        COUNT(CASE WHEN delivery_variance_days > 5 THEN 1 END) * 100.0 / COUNT(*) AS significantly_late_pct,
        
        -- Fill rate reliability
        AVG(fill_rate) AS avg_fill_rate,
        MIN(fill_rate) AS worst_fill_rate,
        
        -- Recommended safety days (statistical)
        -- Safety days = Average lateness + (2 * standard deviation of lead time)
        AVG(delivery_variance_days) + (2 * STDDEV(actual_lead_time_days)) AS recommended_safety_days
        
    FROM lead_time_calculation
    
    GROUP BY ndc_code, supplier_id
),
current_inventory_needs AS (
    -- Join with current inventory position (from previous example)
    SELECT 
        '00093-1058-01' AS ndc_code, 1500 AS current_stock, 45.5 AS daily_demand UNION ALL
    SELECT '00093-2748-01', 800, 120.0 UNION ALL
    SELECT '61755-001-01', 50, 5.2 UNION ALL
    SELECT '00078-0429-61', 25, 2.1
),
ordering_timeline AS (
    SELECT 
        sp.ndc_code,
        sp.supplier_id,
        cin.current_stock,
        cin.daily_demand,
        
        -- Lead time metrics
        ROUND(sp.avg_actual_lead_time, 1) AS standard_lead_time,
        ROUND(sp.lead_time_stddev, 1) AS lead_time_variability,
        ROUND(sp.avg_lateness_days, 1) AS typical_delay,
        ROUND(sp.on_time_delivery_pct, 1) AS reliability_score,
        
        -- Safety calculation
        CEIL(sp.recommended_safety_days) AS safety_stock_days_to_add,
        
        -- Total lead time to plan for
        CEIL(sp.avg_actual_lead_time + GREATEST(sp.recommended_safety_days, 0)) AS planned_lead_time,
        
        -- Days until stockout
        ROUND(cin.current_stock / NULLIF(cin.daily_demand, 0), 1) AS days_until_stockout,
        
        -- Order trigger date (must account for lead time + safety)
        DATEADD(DAY, -CEIL(sp.avg_actual_lead_time + GREATEST(sp.recommended_safety_days, 0)), 
                DATEADD(DAY, CEIL(cin.current_stock / NULLIF(cin.daily_demand, 0)), CURRENT_DATE())) AS latest_order_date,
        
        -- Risk assessment
        CASE 
            WHEN sp.significantly_late_pct > 20 THEN 'HIGH_RISK_SUPPLIER_BUFFER_REQUIRED'
            WHEN sp.avg_fill_rate < 0.95 THEN 'PARTIAL_SHIPMENT_RISK_ORDER_EXTRA'
            WHEN sp.lead_time_stddev > sp.avg_actual_lead_time * 0.3 THEN 'UNRELIABLE_LEAD_TIME_OVERSTOCK_RECOMMENDED'
            ELSE 'RELIABLE_SUPPLIER_STANDARD_BUFFER'
        END AS supplier_risk_profile
        
    FROM supplier_performance sp
    JOIN current_inventory_needs cin ON sp.ndc_code = cin.ndc_code
)
SELECT 
    ndc_code,
    supplier_id,
    current_stock AS on_hand_units,
    daily_demand AS daily_usage_rate,
    days_until_stockout AS stockout_countdown_days,
    
    -- Lead time planning
    standard_lead_time AS typical_delivery_days,
    lead_time_variability AS delivery_window_uncertainty,
    typical_delay AS avg_days_late,
    reliability_score AS pct_on_time,
    
    -- Safety recommendations
    safety_stock_days_to_add AS extra_days_safety_stock,
    planned_lead_time AS total_days_to_plan_for,
    
    -- Critical dates
    latest_order_date AS order_by_date_to_avoid_stockout,
    CURRENT_DATE AS today,
    DATEDIFF(DAY, CURRENT_DATE(), latest_order_date) AS days_until_must_order,
    
    -- Action
    CASE 
        WHEN DATEDIFF(DAY, CURRENT_DATE(), latest_order_date) <= 0 
        THEN 'URGENT_PLACE_ORDER_IMMEDIATELY'
        WHEN DATEDIFF(DAY, CURRENT_DATE(), latest_order_date) <= 2 
        THEN 'PRIORITY_ORDER_THIS_WEEK'
        WHEN DATEDIFF(DAY, CURRENT_DATE(), latest_order_date) <= 7 
        THEN 'PLAN_ORDER_NEXT_WEEK'
        ELSE 'MONITOR_INVENTORY_LEVELS'
    END AS ordering_urgency,
    
    supplier_risk_profile AS risk_mitigation_strategy
    
FROM ordering_timeline

ORDER BY days_until_must_order ASC;
```

**Workflow Explanation**:

1. **Lead Time Calculation** - `DATEDIFF` between order date and actual delivery (not promised)
2. **Variance Analysis** - Standard deviation of lead times (predictability measure)
3. **Safety Days Formula** - Adds buffer for average lateness + 2×standard deviation (statistical safety)
4. **Latest Order Date** - Back-calculates from stockout date minus total lead time (deadline management)
5. **Risk Profiling** - Identifies suppliers with >20% significant delays (silent failure prevention)

## Use Case 5: JIT (Just-In-Time) for High-Cost LAD Drugs

**Scenario**: Minimize inventory holding costs for expensive Limited Access Drugs using JIT ordering triggers.

```sql
-- Create LAD inventory with high holding costs
CREATE OR REPLACE TABLE lad_inventory_jit (
    ndc_code VARCHAR(11),
    drug_name VARCHAR(100),
    current_inventory INTEGER,
    in_transit INTEGER,
    unit_cost DECIMAL(12,2),
    daily_holding_cost_pct DECIMAL(5,4), -- Usually 0.0003 (0.03% daily)
    
    -- Patient pipeline (known future demand)
    confirmed_orders_next_7_days INTEGER,
    confirmed_orders_next_30_days INTEGER,
    pending_authorizations INTEGER,
    
    -- Supply constraints
    manufacturer_lead_time_days INTEGER,
    minimum_order_quantity INTEGER,
    temperature_requirements VARCHAR(20),
    requires_refrigeration BOOLEAN
);

-- Insert high-cost specialty drugs
INSERT INTO lad_inventory_jit VALUES
('61755-001-01', 'Revlimid 5mg', 28, 0, 850.00, 0.0003, 14, 56, 12, 14, 28, 'controlled_room', FALSE),
('61755-001-02', 'Revlimid 10mg', 21, 21, 1200.00, 0.0003, 7, 28, 8, 14, 21, 'controlled_room', FALSE),
('00078-0429-61', 'Harvoni', 14, 0, 1200.00, 0.0005, 3, 12, 5, 21, 14, 'refrigerated', TRUE),
('00078-0430-61', 'Sovaldi', 7, 14, 1100.00, 0.0005, 2, 8, 3, 21, 7, 'refrigerated', TRUE),
('61755-002-01', 'Pomalyst 4mg', 21, 0, 1850.00, 0.0003, 7, 21, 6, 10, 21, 'controlled_room', FALSE);

-- ORDERING QUERY: JIT optimization for high-value drugs
WITH holding_cost_analysis AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Daily holding cost per unit
        unit_cost * daily_holding_cost_pct AS daily_holding_cost_per_unit,
        
        -- Total inventory value
        (current_inventory + in_transit) * unit_cost AS total_inventory_value,
        
        -- Daily holding cost for current position
        (current_inventory + in_transit) * unit_cost * daily_holding_cost_pct AS daily_carrying_cost,
        
        -- Annual holding cost projection
        (current_inventory + in_transit) * unit_cost * daily_holding_cost_pct * 365 AS annual_holding_cost
        
    FROM lad_inventory_jit
),
demand_coverage AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Days of coverage by current inventory
        CASE 
            WHEN confirmed_orders_next_7_days > 0 
            THEN current_inventory * 1.0 / (confirmed_orders_next_7_days / 7.0)
            ELSE 999
        END AS days_coverage_current_stock,
        
        -- Pipeline coverage (including in-transit)
        (current_inventory + in_transit) * 1.0 / 
            NULLIF(confirmed_orders_next_30_days / 30.0, 0) AS days_coverage_pipeline,
        
        -- JIT trigger: Order when coverage falls below lead time + 2 days buffer
        manufacturer_lead_time_days + 2 AS jit_target_coverage_days
        
    FROM holding_cost_analysis
),
jit_ordering_logic AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- JIT Order Quantity: Cover next 30 days of confirmed orders minus pipeline
        GREATEST(
            confirmed_orders_next_30_days - (current_inventory + in_transit),
            minimum_order_quantity  -- Respect MOQ
        ) AS jit_order_quantity,
        
        -- Order trigger flag
        CASE 
            WHEN days_coverage_pipeline <= jit_target_coverage_days 
            THEN 'PLACE_JIT_ORDER_NOW'
            WHEN days_coverage_pipeline <= jit_target_coverage_days + 3 
            THEN 'PREPARE_JIT_ORDER'
            ELSE 'ADEQUATE_JIT_COVERAGE'
        END AS jit_status,
        
        -- Financial impact of ordering vs. not ordering
        -- Cost of ordering now: Holding cost for excess inventory
        -- Cost of delaying: Risk of stockout (patient care impact)
        CASE 
            WHEN days_coverage_pipeline < manufacturer_lead_time_days 
            THEN 'RISK_PATIENT_CARE_STOCKOUT'
            WHEN days_coverage_pipeline < manufacturer_lead_time_days + 2 
            THEN 'OPTIMAL_JIT_WINDOW'
            WHEN days_coverage_pipeline > manufacturer_lead_time_days + 14 
            THEN 'EXCESS_INVENTORY_REDUCE_ORDERS'
        END AS inventory_position_assessment
        
    FROM demand_coverage
),
financial_optimization AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Order value
        jit_order_quantity * unit_cost AS order_investment,
        
        -- Holding cost if we order now (carry for lead time + 30 days avg)
        (jit_order_quantity * unit_cost * daily_holding_cost_pct * 
         (manufacturer_lead_time_days + 15)) AS estimated_holding_cost_for_order,
        
        -- Savings vs. ordering 30 days supply blindly
        ((current_inventory + in_transit) * unit_cost * daily_holding_cost_pct * 30) - 
        ((GREATEST(confirmed_orders_next_7_days - current_inventory, 0) + confirmed_orders_next_30_days - confirmed_orders_next_7_days) 
         * unit_cost * daily_holding_cost_pct * 15) AS jit_savings_vs_bulk_order,
        
        -- Total cost of ownership (inventory + ordering)
        (current_inventory + in_transit + jit_order_quantity) * unit_cost * daily_holding_cost_pct * 365 AS projected_annual_tco
        
    FROM jit_ordering_logic
)
SELECT 
    ndc_code,
    drug_name,
    
    -- Current position
    current_inventory AS units_on_hand,
    in_transit AS units_inbound,
    total_inventory_value AS inventory_investment,
    
    -- Holding costs
    ROUND(daily_carrying_cost, 2) AS cost_to_carry_per_day,
    ROUND(annual_holding_cost, 2) AS annual_carrying_cost,
    
    -- Demand visibility
    confirmed_orders_next_7_days AS known_demand_7d,
    confirmed_orders_next_30_days AS known_demand_30d,
    pending_authorizations AS potential_additional_demand,
    
    -- Coverage analysis
    ROUND(days_coverage_pipeline, 1) AS days_of_supply_remaining,
    jit_target_coverage_days AS minimum_coverage_target,
    
    -- JIT recommendation
    jit_order_quantity AS recommended_order_qty,
    ROUND(order_investment, 2) AS order_value,
    jit_status AS action_required,
    inventory_position_assessment AS strategy,
    
    -- Financial benefits
    ROUND(estimated_holding_cost_for_order, 2) AS holding_cost_for_new_order,
    ROUND(jit_savings_vs_bulk_order, 2) AS estimated_annual_savings_vs_bulk,
    
    -- Constraints
    minimum_order_quantity AS moq_constraint,
    manufacturer_lead_time_days AS supply_lead_time,
    temperature_requirements AS storage_requirement,
    
    -- Compliance warning (silent failure mode)
    CASE 
        WHEN requires_refrigeration AND days_coverage_pipeline > 30 
        THEN 'WARNING_REFRIGERATED_EXCESS_STOCK_EXPIRY_RISK'
        WHEN pending_authorizations > current_inventory * 0.5 AND in_transit = 0
        THEN 'WARNING_AUTH_PIPELINE_EXCEEDS_STOCK_ORDER_NOW'
        WHEN confirmed_orders_next_7_days > current_inventory AND in_transit = 0
        THEN 'CRITICAL_INSUFFICIENT_STOCK_FOR_CONFIRMED_ORDERS'
        ELSE 'JIT_POSITION_OPTIMAL'
    END AS risk_alert
    
FROM financial_optimization

ORDER BY total_inventory_value DESC;
```

**Workflow Explanation**:

1. **Holding Cost Calculation** - Daily cost of carrying inventory (0.03% of value for specialty drugs)
2. **Demand Visibility** - Uses confirmed orders (not forecasts) for JIT precision (patient-specific)
3. **Coverage Ratio** - Days of supply based on actual confirmed demand (not historical average)
4. **JIT Trigger** - Orders when coverage ≤ lead time + 2 days (minimal buffer)
5. **TCO Optimization** - Compares JIT savings vs. bulk ordering (capital efficiency)

## Use Case 6: Multi-Echelon Supply Chain (Warehouse to Pharmacy)

**Scenario**: Coordinate ordering between central warehouse (852) and retail pharmacies (840/retail).

```sql
-- Create multi-echelon inventory data
CREATE OR REPLACE TABLE multi_echelon_network (
    echelon_level VARCHAR(20), -- 'central', 'regional', 'retail'
    location_id VARCHAR(20),
    parent_location_id VARCHAR(20),
    ndc_code VARCHAR(11),
    current_stock INTEGER,
    reorder_point INTEGER,
    economic_order_qty INTEGER,
    daily_demand_rate DECIMAL(10,2),
    lead_time_from_parent_days INTEGER
);

-- Insert network structure
INSERT INTO multi_echelon_network VALUES
-- Central warehouse (supplies regions)
('central', 'WH_CENTRAL', NULL, '00093-1058-01', 50000, 10000, 20000, 500.0, 14),
('central', 'WH_CENTRAL', NULL, '00093-2748-01', 30000, 6000, 15000, 300.0, 14),

-- Regional warehouses (supply pharmacies)
('regional', 'WH_EAST', 'WH_CENTRAL', '00093-1058-01', 5000, 1000, 2500, 50.0, 3),
('regional', 'WH_EAST', 'WH_CENTRAL', '00093-2748-01', 3000, 600, 1500, 30.0, 3),
('regional', 'WH_WEST', 'WH_CENTRAL', '00093-1058-01', 4000, 800, 2000, 40.0, 5),

-- Retail pharmacies (end customers)
('retail', 'PHARM_001', 'WH_EAST', '00093-1058-01', 150, 30, 100, 15.0, 1),
('retail', 'PHARM_002', 'WH_EAST', '00093-1058-01', 120, 25, 80, 12.0, 1),
('retail', 'PHARM_003', 'WH_WEST', '00093-1058-01', 80, 20, 60, 8.0, 2),
('retail', 'PHARM_001', 'WH_EAST', '00093-2748-01', 200, 40, 120, 20.0, 1);

-- ORDERING QUERY: Multi-echelon ordering coordination
WITH echelon_demand_rollup AS (
    -- Calculate dependent demand at each level (retail demand rolls up to regional, etc.)
    SELECT 
        parent_location_id,
        ndc_code,
        SUM(current_stock) AS downstream_total_stock,
        SUM(reorder_point) AS downstream_total_reorder_points,
        SUM(daily_demand_rate) AS downstream_aggregate_demand,
        COUNT(*) AS downstream_location_count
    FROM multi_echelon_network
    WHERE echelon_level = 'retail'
    GROUP BY parent_location_id, ndc_code
),
echelon_analysis AS (
    SELECT 
        me.*,
        
        -- Days of supply at this echelon
        CASE 
            WHEN daily_demand_rate > 0 THEN current_stock / daily_demand_rate
            ELSE 999
        END AS local_days_supply,
        
        -- Total network position
        COALESCE(edr.downstream_total_stock, 0) + current_stock AS echelon_network_inventory,
        
        -- Combined demand rate
        COALESCE(edr.downstream_aggregate_demand, daily_demand_rate) AS total_echelon_demand
        
    FROM multi_echelon_network me
    LEFT JOIN echelon_demand_rollup edr 
        ON me.location_id = edr.parent_location_id AND me.ndc_code = edr.ndc_code
),
ordering_priorities AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Order urgency by echelon (retail orders first, then propagate up)
        CASE echelon_level
            WHEN 'retail' THEN 
                CASE 
                    WHEN current_stock <= reorder_point * 0.5 THEN 1
                    WHEN current_stock <= reorder_point THEN 2
                    ELSE 5
                END
            WHEN 'regional' THEN 
                CASE 
                    WHEN current_stock <= (downstream_total_reorder_points * 2) THEN 3
                    WHEN current_stock <= downstream_total_stock THEN 4
                    ELSE 6
                END
            ELSE 7  -- Central
        END AS echelon_priority,
        
        -- Quantity to order (different logic per echelon)
        CASE echelon_level
            WHEN 'retail' THEN 
                GREATEST(economic_order_qty, reorder_point * 2 - current_stock)
            WHEN 'regional' THEN 
                GREATEST(economic_order_qty, downstream_total_reorder_points * 3 - current_stock)
            ELSE 
                GREATEST(economic_order_qty, 10000)  -- Central has bulk MOQ
        END AS suggested_order_quantity,
        
        -- Risk of stockout propagating up
        CASE 
            WHEN echelon_level = 'regional' AND current_stock < downstream_total_reorder_points 
            THEN 'CRITICAL_DOWNSTREAM_STOCKOUT_RISK'
            WHEN echelon_level = 'central' AND current_stock < (SELECT SUM(reorder_point) FROM multi_echelon_network WHERE echelon_level = 'regional' AND ndc_code = ea.ndc_code)
            THEN 'NETWORK_INVENTORY_CRITICAL'
            ELSE 'ECHELON_STABLE'
        END AS network_risk_status
        
    FROM echelon_analysis ea
)
SELECT 
    echelon_level AS supply_chain_level,
    location_id,
    parent_location_id AS supplied_by,
    ndc_code,
    
    -- Inventory position
    current_stock AS on_hand_units,
    reorder_point AS reorder_threshold,
    ROUND(local_days_supply, 1) AS days_of_supply,
    
    -- Network context
    COALESCE(downstream_location_count, 0) AS locations_served,
    COALESCE(downstream_total_stock, 0) AS downstream_inventory,
    
    -- Demand
    daily_demand_rate AS local_daily_demand,
    COALESCE(downstream_aggregate_demand, daily_demand_rate) AS total_demand_influenced,
    
    -- Ordering
    suggested_order_quantity AS recommended_order,
    echelon_priority AS urgency_rank,
    network_risk_status AS system_health,
    
    -- Action
    CASE 
        WHEN echelon_level = 'retail' AND current_stock <= reorder_point 
        THEN 'PLACE_ORDER_TO_REGIONAL'
        WHEN echelon_level = 'regional' AND current_stock <= downstream_total_reorder_points 
        THEN 'URGENT_ORDER_TO_CENTRAL'
        WHEN echelon_level = 'central' AND current_stock < (SELECT SUM(reorder_point)*2 FROM multi_echelon_network WHERE echelon_level = 'regional' AND ndc_code = op.ndc_code)
        THEN 'PLACE_BULK_ORDER_TO_SUPPLIER'
        ELSE 'NO_ACTION_MONITOR'
    END AS recommended_action,
    
    -- Silent failure: Regional looks good but retail is starving
    CASE 
        WHEN echelon_level = 'regional' AND current_stock > reorder_point * 2 AND 
             EXISTS (SELECT 1 FROM multi_echelon_network retail 
                    WHERE retail.parent_location_id = op.location_id 
                    AND retail.current_stock < retail.reorder_point * 0.5)
        THEN 'ALERT_ALLOCATION_FAILURE_RETAIL_STARVING'
        WHEN echelon_level = 'central' AND current_stock > 10000 AND
             (SELECT SUM(current_stock) FROM multi_echelon_network r 
              WHERE r.ndc_code = op.ndc_code AND r.echelon_level = 'regional') < 1000
        THEN 'ALERT_DISTRIBUTION_BREAKDOWN'
        ELSE 'FLOWS_BALANCED'
    END AS allocation_integrity
    
FROM ordering_priorities op

ORDER BY echelon_priority, ndc_code, echelon_level;
```

**Workflow Explanation**:

1. **Echelon Rollup** - Aggregates retail demand to regional level (dependent demand calculation)
2. **Network Inventory** - Sums stock across all levels for total system visibility
3. **Priority Logic** - Retail gets priority 1-2, regional 3-4, central 7 (cascading replenishment)
4. **Risk Propagation** - Identifies when regional stock is insufficient for retail reorder points
5. **Allocation Integrity** - Detects when central has stock but regional/retail are starving (distribution breakdown)

```sql
-- ORDERING QUERY: Accuracy and timeliness validation
WITH accuracy_checks AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Mathematical accuracy validation
        CASE 
            WHEN ABS(total_amount - calculated_total) > 0.01 THEN 'MATHEMATICAL_ERROR'
            ELSE 'MATH_ACCURATE'
        END AS math_accuracy_status,
        ABS(total_amount - calculated_total) AS math_discrepancy_amount,
        
        -- Price accuracy (unit price × qty should equal total)
        CASE 
            WHEN ABS((quantity_ordered * unit_price) - total_amount) > 0.01 THEN 'PRICE_CALCULATION_ERROR'
            ELSE 'PRICE_ACCURATE'
        END AS price_calculation_status,
        
        -- Financial capacity check
        CASE 
            WHEN (buyer_current_balance + total_amount) > buyer_credit_limit THEN 'CREDIT_EXCEEDED'
            ELSE 'WITHIN_CREDIT_LIMIT'
        END AS credit_status,
        buyer_credit_limit - buyer_current_balance AS available_credit,
        
        -- Inventory availability at time of order
        CASE 
            WHEN quantity_ordered > available_inventory_at_order_time THEN 'INSUFFICIENT_SUPPLIER_STOCK'
            ELSE 'STOCK_AVAILABLE'
        END AS inventory_availability,
        
        -- Timeliness checks
        CASE 
            WHEN DATE(order_date) > quote_expiration_date THEN 'QUOTE_EXPIRED'
            ELSE 'QUOTE_VALID'
        END AS quote_timeliness,
        DATEDIFF(DAY, quote_expiration_date, DATE(order_date)) AS days_past_expiration,
        
        -- Cutoff time compliance
        CASE 
            WHEN actual_order_time > order_cutoff_time THEN 'MISSED_CUTOFF_NEXT_DAY'
            ELSE 'WITHIN_CUTOFF'
        END AS cutoff_compliance,
        
        -- Delivery feasibility
        CASE 
            WHEN requested_delivery_date < DATEADD(DAY, 1, DATE(order_date)) AND actual_order_time > order_cutoff_time 
            THEN 'DELIVERY_IMPOSSIBLE_CUTOFF_MISSED'
            WHEN requested_delivery_date < DATEADD(DAY, 2, DATE(order_date)) 
            THEN 'RUSH_DELIVERY_REQUIRED'
            ELSE 'DELIVERY_FEASIBLE'
        END AS delivery_feasibility
        
    FROM edi_840_orders
),
quality_score AS (
    SELECT 
        *,
        
        -- Composite accuracy score (0-100)
        (CASE WHEN math_accuracy_status = 'MATH_ACCURATE' THEN 25 ELSE 0 END +
         CASE WHEN price_calculation_status = 'PRICE_ACCURATE' THEN 25 ELSE 0 END +
         CASE WHEN credit_status = 'WITHIN_CREDIT_LIMIT' THEN 20 ELSE 0 END +
         CASE WHEN quote_timeliness = 'QUOTE_VALID' THEN 15 ELSE 0 END +
         CASE WHEN cutoff_compliance = 'WITHIN_CUTOFF' THEN 15 ELSE 0 END) AS order_quality_score,
        
        -- Critical error flag (any accuracy violation)
        CASE 
            WHEN math_accuracy_status = 'MATHEMATICAL_ERROR' THEN 'CRITICAL_ACCURACY_FAILURE'
            WHEN price_calculation_status = 'PRICE_CALCULATION_ERROR' THEN 'CRITICAL_PRICING_ERROR'
            WHEN credit_status = 'CREDIT_EXCEEDED' THEN 'CRITICAL_CREDIT_VIOLATION'
            WHEN quote_timeliness = 'QUOTE_EXPIRED' THEN 'CRITICAL_TIMELINESS_FAILURE'
            WHEN inventory_availability = 'INSUFFICIENT_SUPPLIER_STOCK' THEN 'CRITICAL_STOCK_SHORTAGE'
            ELSE 'NO_CRITICAL_ERRORS'
        END AS critical_error_type,
        
        -- Business impact assessment
        CASE 
            WHEN order_status = 'rejected' AND rejection_reason IS NOT NULL THEN 'ORDER_FAILED_PROCESSING_DELAYED'
            WHEN order_status = 'pending' AND cutoff_compliance = 'MISSED_CUTOFF_NEXT_DAY' THEN 'DELIVERY_DELAY_CUSTOMER_IMPACT'
            WHEN math_accuracy_status = 'MATHEMATICAL_ERROR' AND order_status = 'accepted' THEN 'FINANCIAL_LOSS_RISK_UNDERCHARGE'
            ELSE 'PROCEED_WITH_FULFILLMENT'
        END AS business_impact
        
    FROM accuracy_checks
)
SELECT 
    order_id,
    ndc_code,
    buyer_id,
    seller_id,
    quantity_ordered AS qty,
    unit_price,
    total_amount AS claimed_total,
    calculated_total AS system_calculated_total,
    
    -- Accuracy metrics
    math_accuracy_status AS math_check,
    math_discrepancy_amount AS discrepancy,
    price_calculation_status AS pricing_check,
    order_quality_score AS accuracy_score_0_100,
    critical_error_type AS severity_classification,
    
    -- Financial validation
    credit_status AS buyer_credit_ok,
    available_credit AS remaining_credit,
    inventory_availability AS stock_available,
    
    -- Timeliness metrics
    quote_timeliness AS quote_still_valid,
    days_past_expiration AS days_late_on_quote,
    cutoff_compliance AS met_order_cutoff,
    actual_order_time AS time_ordered,
    order_cutoff_time AS deadline,
    
    -- Delivery impact
    requested_delivery_date AS customer_wants_delivery_by,
    delivery_feasibility AS can_meet_date,
    
    -- Final disposition
    order_status AS current_status,
    rejection_reason AS why_rejected,
    business_impact AS operational_impact,
    
    -- Silent failure detection (appears OK but isn't)
    CASE 
        WHEN order_status = 'accepted' AND math_accuracy_status = 'MATHEMATICAL_ERROR' 
        THEN 'SILENT_FAILURE_ACCEPTED_WITH_MATH_ERROR'
        WHEN order_status = 'accepted' AND inventory_availability = 'INSUFFICIENT_SUPPLIER_STOCK'
        THEN 'SILENT_FAILURE_ACCEPTED_NO_STOCK'
        WHEN order_status = 'pending' AND DATEDIFF(HOUR, order_date, CURRENT_TIMESTAMP()) > 24
        THEN 'SILENT_FAILURE_STUCK_PENDING_INVESTIGATE'
        ELSE 'NO_SILENT_FAILURES'
    END AS data_integrity_alert
    
FROM quality_score

ORDER BY 
    CASE critical_error_type 
        WHEN 'CRITICAL_ACCURACY_FAILURE' THEN 1
        WHEN 'CRITICAL_PRICING_ERROR' THEN 2
        WHEN 'CRITICAL_CREDIT_VIOLATION' THEN 3
        WHEN 'CRITICAL_STOCK_SHORTAGE' THEN 4
        WHEN 'CRITICAL_TIMELINESS_FAILURE' THEN 5
        ELSE 6
    END,
    order_quality_score ASC;
```

**Workflow Explanation**:

1. **Mathematical Validation** - Compares claimed total vs. calculated (qty × price) (prevents under/over charging)
2. **Credit Validation** - Ensures buyer has available credit (prevents bad debt)
3. **Timeliness Checks** - Validates quote expiration and cutoff times (prevents delivery delays)
4. **Quality Scoring** - 0-100 score based on accuracy factors (supplier performance metric)
5. **Silent Failure Detection** - Identifies accepted orders with math errors or no stock (system failures)

## Key Ordering Concepts Summary

| Concept | Business Rule | Pharma Application | SQL Implementation |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Reorder Point** | (Avg Demand × Lead Time) + Safety Stock | Prevent drug stockouts | `CASE WHEN net_position <= rop THEN 'ORDER'` |
| **FIFO** | First Expired, First Out | Minimize drug waste | `ROW_NUMBER() OVER (ORDER BY expiration_date)` |
| **Safety Stock** | (Max - Avg) × Lead Time × Reliability Factor | Buffer for variability | Statistical calculation with `STDDEV` |
| **JIT** | Order when coverage ≤ Lead Time + Buffer | High-cost drug management | Demand visibility + minimal buffer |
| **Lead Time** | Actual delivery - Order date (not promised) | Accurate planning | `DATEDIFF` on historical POs |
| **Multi-Echelon** | Retail demand rolls up to Regional | Supply chain coordination | Recursive CTE or self-join rollup |

**Critical Ordering Validation Rules**:

1. **Accuracy** - Unit Price × Quantity = Total Amount (mathematical conservation)
2. **Timeliness** - Order Date ≤ Quote Expiration (valid contract)
3. **Feasibility** - Requested Delivery ≥ Order Date + Lead Time (physical possibility)
4. **Credit** - Current Balance + Order ≤ Credit Limit (financial capacity)
5. **Inventory** - Order Quantity ≤ Available + In Transit - Allocated (allocation integrity)

**Silent Failure Modes in Ordering**:

| Failure | Appearance | Reality | Detection |
|---------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Phantom Inventory** | System shows stock | Reserved/Quarantined/Expired | `on_hand - reserved` vs `available` |
| **Quote Expiration** | Order accepted | Price no longer valid | `order_date > quote_expiry` |
| **Math Errors** | Total looks correct | Qty × Price ≠ Total | `ABS(calculated - claimed) > 0.01` |
| **Lead Time Ignorance** | Stock looks adequate | Delivery takes longer than remaining supply | `days_supply < lead_time` |
| **Bulk Assumption** | Larger orders save money | Holding costs exceed savings | `holding_cost > purchase_discount` |

**Remember**: Just like the seasonal clothing example where ordering too early creates holding costs or too late creates stockouts, pharmaceutical ordering must balance **accuracy** (right drug, right quantity) with **timeliness** (before stockout, after quote valid) to ensure patient care continuity and financial efficiency!

> [!Tip]
> Implementing a just-in-time (JIT) ordering system can help minimize inventory costs and maximize customer satisfaction by ensuring that products are available when needed without excess stock.

## Key Points
- Define clear ordering thresholds to trigger new orders and prevent stockouts.
- Implement a first-in, first-out (FIFO) inventory system to reduce waste and obsolescence.
- Use data analytics to forecast demand and adjust ordering quantities accordingly.
- Establish strong relationships with suppliers to negotiate better terms and improve delivery times.
- Consider implementing a drop shipping model for products that are not core to your business.

> [!important]
> The one rule that must not be violated is to always prioritize accuracy and timeliness in the ordering process, as mistakes can lead to significant delays and additional costs.

## Mental Model
1. **Demand Forecasting**: Analyze historical sales data and market trends to predict future demand.
2. **Inventory Management**: Use the forecasted demand to determine the optimal inventory levels and ordering quantities.
3. **Order Placement**: Place orders with suppliers based on the determined quantities and lead times.

## Observe
- A common mistake people make is failing to account for lead times and supply chain disruptions when placing orders.
- A silent failure mode is when inventory levels appear adequate but are actually insufficient due to stock being tied up in transit or pending quality control.
- A misleading assumption is that ordering in bulk always leads to cost savings, as it can sometimes result in unnecessary inventory holding costs.

> [!Important]
> For example, a retail business that sells seasonal clothing can apply the principles of effective ordering by forecasting demand based on historical sales data, managing inventory levels to meet that demand, and placing orders with suppliers well in advance of the peak season to avoid stockouts and ensure timely delivery.
