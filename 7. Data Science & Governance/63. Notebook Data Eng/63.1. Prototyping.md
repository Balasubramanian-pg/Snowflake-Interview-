# What is Prototyping and How Does it Enhance Product Development

> [!Note]
> Prototyping is a crucial step in product development that involves creating a preliminary model of a product to test and refine its design, functionality, and usability.

## Why This Matters
- Effective prototyping supports the decision of whether to proceed with a product idea, saving time and resources by identifying potential flaws early on.
- Misunderstanding the importance of prototyping can lead to launching a product that fails to meet user needs, resulting in significant financial losses and damage to a company's reputation.
- You would look up information on prototyping when developing a new product, especially during the initial stages of the design process, to ensure that your product meets the required standards and user expectations.

> [!Tip]
> Start by creating a low-fidelity prototype to quickly test and validate your product idea before investing in high-fidelity prototypes or the final product.

## Key Points
- Prototyping helps identify and fix design and functionality issues early in the product development process.
- It enables developers to gather feedback from users and stakeholders, which can inform design decisions and improvements.
- Prototyping can be applied to various aspects of product development, including user interface (UI), user experience (UX), and core functionality.
- Different types of prototypes, such as paper prototypes, digital prototypes, and functional prototypes, serve different purposes and are used at various stages of the development process.

> [!important]
> The one invariant that must not be violated in prototyping is the iterative process of testing, feedback, and refinement, ensuring that the prototype evolves based on user input and technical feasibility.

## Mental Model
- Step 1: Define the product idea and identify the key features to be prototyped.
- Step 2: Create a prototype based on the defined features, starting with low-fidelity and progressing to high-fidelity as necessary.
- Step 3: Test the prototype with users and stakeholders, gather feedback, and refine the design and functionality accordingly.

## Observe
- A common mistake people make is skipping the prototyping phase or not testing prototypes with real users, leading to products that do not meet user needs.
- A silent failure mode in prototyping is the assumption that a prototype is final or that it perfectly represents the intended product, which can lead to overlooking critical issues.
- A misleading assumption is that prototyping is only about creating a visually appealing product, when in fact, it is equally about functionality, usability, and the overall user experience.

> [!Important]
> For example, a company developing a new smartphone app might create a paper prototype to test the app's layout and navigation, then progress to a digital prototype to test its interactive elements, finally refining the app based on user feedback before launch.
